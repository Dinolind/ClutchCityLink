How Long Can the Houston Rockets’ Core Stay Together? (+ Who Will Be Traded First)

We see it happen year after year. Teams around the organization eventually come to the conclusion that keeping their core together isn’t the best idea moving forward. The Cavs and Hawks did it last season. Before them, it was the Nets, Suns, Kings, and Bulls. Every team has gone through the process of trading a key, young piece at one point or another. For Houston, this day might still be a few years off, or it could be a few months off. However, the reality is that it’s going to come one way or another. The big question is when and who? What follows is my best attempt at an answer based on the information currently available.

Who?

The way I see it, the Rockets have 4 main guys that would be actively considered integral parts of this roster: Jalen Green, Jabari Smith Jr., Alperen Sengun, and, presumably, Amen Thompson. The last one can really be replaced by whoever the team ends up selecting in the NBA Draft on June 22nd. The rest of the roster is good, don’t get me wrong, but they don’t have the same level of internal value as those 4 players. I think it’s safe to say that Jalen Green won’t be the one to go first nor will it be Amen Thompson. Jalen is the face of the franchise, the team leader, and the highest drafted prospect of the group. Furthermore, Thompson isn’t even technically in the NBA yet, so I doubt he’ll even be in the running if something happens sooner, rather than later.

That leaves Jabari Smith Jr. and Alperen Sengun. While the obvious answer may seem like Smith because of his underwhelming rookie season, I’m actually going to pick Sengun. Sengun isn’t a bad player by any stretch of the imagination, which is exactly why I think he’ll be one traded when a tough decision inevitably has to be made. Just envision a scenario where a disgruntled all-star wants to be moved to a different team. The team trading him is, almost certainly, going to want a future building block in return. When you look at these two players, Sengun seems much more like a “building block” than Jabari, who feels like more of a complementary piece. Additionally, the former Turkish-league MVP has shown more flashes of greatness and appears to have a higher ceiling than Smith, which is more appealing to potential trade partners.

When?

Now that I’ve established that it’s most likely to be Alperen Sengun traded first from the main group of players, the big question is when it will happen. The Rockets are in such a scenario where no one really knows, but we can still take a guess. First off, I doubt he will get traded this off-season. From everything we’ve seen regarding new head coach Ime Udoka, Alpi is consistently brought up as a point of emphasis. There also haven’t been very many rumors pointing towards Houston looking to go after a new center, different from how it’s been with the rest of the positions. The only two names to even be mentioned so far are Brook Lopez and Jarace Walker, but both of those guys would be brought in as a good fit alongside Sengun.

My best guess is that it’ll happen either at this upcoming deadline or during the following offseason. This team next season is either going to surprise a lot of people in a positive way or ultimately crumble due to a lack of experience. In the first scenario, Rafael Stone may make a somewhat questionable move in an attempt to secure a spot in the postseason. In the other, Stone will likely feel forced to succumb to the pressure and trade one of his young assets for a veteran piece to avoid yet another disappointing year in 2024-2025. Of course, no one knows for sure, and there’s still a good chance it takes a lot longer than that. However, the expectation for this roster to win games may come a lot sooner than you think.

Final Thoughts

The Rockets organization is ready to compete. More specifically, Tilman Fertita is ready to compete. The time is coming soon when something major is going to happen, and the decisions made will have consequences. It’s not easy building a championship contender because it takes precise timing paired with a bit of luck and knowledge from the front office. Trades exist for a reason: it’s nearly impossible to go from bad to good without them. I can’t stress enough how much the Rockets’ roster is going to change over the next year and a half or so. We have the foundation, yet we’re nowhere near the ultimate goal. All we can do now as fans is hope. Hope that everything turns out the way it should.
