Why Waiving Justin Holiday and Danny Green Post-Deadline Was a Bad Idea

Two of the pieces that the Houston Rockets acquired at the trade deadline have already been waived and signed by a new team: Danny Green and Justin Holiday. At first glance, this news seems like pretty much nothing, but there are a lot more implications than you may expect. Of course, we all knew John Wall was going to be bought out yet again when the Eric Gordon trade was made, but with Green gone as well, we basically traded the former 6th man of the year for a chance at moving up 5-10 spots in the draft this season. Doesn’t look like all that great of a return anymore. The other trade they made including Garrison Mathews and Bruno Fernando looks worse with Holiday out of the picture as well.

What It Means

This team desperately needed a new veteran presence, and now it’s even worse. The turnovers are simply inexcusable, which is mainly because there’s not one person who knows how to take care of the basketball at the end of close games. That part doesn’t even matter, though, if they can’t play any defense to keep the matchup close in the first place. Since the deadline, the Rockets have already given up over 120 points twice and now the entire league is going to be off for a week, which will likely make opposing offenses even better. I’m not saying we need to go all in on vets. In fact, I’d much prefer the opposite, but player development is especially difficult when there’s not a presence in the locker room who has plenty of experience.

It’s also concerning that it appears on the outside as if players with history in the NBA don’t want to play for Houston. John Wall said how poorly everything is run over here, and this could be another red flag. Rafael Stone expressed interest in keeping Green and Holiday at his post-trade press conference, but he wasn’t willing to offer either of them (presumably for Holiday) an extension causing both to seek buyouts. Is he sending mixed signals? That just doesn’t sit right with me. Banking on signing a whole bunch of quality players this off-season just isn’t the right strategy in my eyes; the process should’ve started a long time ago. Even this off-season we’re likely going to have to overpay people for them even to come here. There’s just no other incentive for them, which is worrisome as a fan. I don’t see how we can be successful if that is indeed the reality we are facing.

Positives

With every situation there are at least a few positives, which I want to point out, so it doesn’t seem like a complete dumpster fire for the Rockets. First off, we did keep Frank Kamisky, who can somewhat help keep this roster together and under control while hopefully improving the Rockets overall shooting percentages. We also saw that Tilman Fertitia isn’t as bad of an owner as many people are led to believe. He has paid plenty of guys tons of money to simply stay away and not interfere with the development of our young talent. Fertita also essentially bought 2 second round picks from Atlanta, even throwing in other cash considerations to make the transaction go through. We should be grateful for an owner to even allow a rebuild in the first place as well. Some would just demand that the roster be as good as it can, whether that means playoffs or not, just to keep selling tickets.

He’s not perfect by any means, but Fertita isn’t all that bad either. It’s time to let go of whatever grudges remain from the 2017-2018 team that had a chance to win it all. Now there’s a new era upon us and I believe our owner is willing to spend what it takes to make the roster the best it can be. Additionally, with no one that has more than 3 years of experience expected to receive consistent playing time, we finally get to see some of the young guys who have been buried in the rotation. If we had kept Holiday and Green, they would almost certainly have taken away some opportunities from players like Josh Chrisopher or Tyty Washington Jr., which probably wouldn’t have been good in the long-run. Lastly, Houston also now has 2 roster spots open that can be used on signing players in free agency. Who knows? Maybe our front office will find the next diamond in the rough.

Final Thoughts

The bottom line is that the Rockets could be in trouble if quality guys don’t want to come here this summer. After all, having a ton of cap-space won’t matter much if you can’t do anything with it. To be honest, I wasn’t really impressed with Stone’s interview overall anyways. He thinks the team is on the right track and that they’ve gotten better based on what they’re trying to accomplish. I’m starting to wonder what that is exactly because it seems like no steps are being taken in the direction towards winning games. Also, he refused to acknowledge that the team was struggling this season. It could be just GM speak, but if he’s really that far in denial then we have bigger issues. The national attention also keeps getting worse, which has to affect the players in some regard. The reality is the remaining portion of this season is, most likely, going to be miserable, and we’re just going to have to wait to see if Stone’s plan actually comes to fruition after the playoffs.
