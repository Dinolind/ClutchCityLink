Aggressive Option the Rockets May Go After To Turn This Team Around Quickly

The NBA playoffs are finally here, and for the Rockets, that means the season is over. Usually, this is a time of sorrow, but instead, this year it is a time for celebration. We have made it through the worst of our rebuild, and it’s all up from here. That being said, there’s still major work to do before this roster is at a somewhat competitive level heading into next season. The way I see it is that there are two options. Option 1 is to play the long game by waiting for our young core to progress and develop while surrounding them with veteran role players for guidance. Option 2, on the other hand, is to trade our young talent now and have a drastic overhaul geared towards success in the immediate future. While I tend to prefer option 1, today I’ll be exploring option 2. Below are 3 somewhat realistic star players, other than James Harden, that could be headed to Houston if the front office decides to go all in.

Jaylen Brown

Jaylen Brown has been by far the player surrounded with the most rumors and speculation regarding the Rockets organization. Ever since the Celtics attempted to trade Brown for Kevin Durant multiple times, many have been led to assume that he may want out of Boston. The Houston connection came from Bill Simmons’ podcast where Houston was name dropped as the place where the former 3rd overall pick wanted to be. This may be a bit of a surprise, but he is in the last year of his contract (after the playoffs) and we certainly have the assets if need be. I think the fit works perfectly too. Just imagine a starting lineup of Scoot Henderson, Jalen Green, Jaylen Brown, Jabari Smith Jr., and whatever big man we can find in free agency. That team just sounds exciting.

Trae Young

Trae Young has also been mentioned by the national media as a star potentially on the move. The Atlanta Hawks have underperformed this season, to an extent, after acquiring Dejounte Murray last offseason. In fact, they are currently playing the Celtics in the first round as the 7th seed. They may come to a realization that a decision needs to be made between Young and Murray if the team ever wants a real chance at winning it all. Who better than Houston to swoop in and offer a trade package that Atlanta would accept. It would probably even cost us less, asset wise, than a deal for Jaylen Brown. The backcourt of him and Jalen Green scares me a bit defensively, but the offense is going to be so good that it may not matter. However, we all said the same thing about Dallas and look how that turned out.

Bradley Beal

I bet everyone feels like a broken record talking about Bradley Beal in trade rumors. He has been stuck on an underwhelming Washington Wizards team for several years at this point, yet still nothing has happened. I don’t know how the fit works alongside Jalen Green, but I think it would be decent with the right point guard to facilitate it all. After all, the NBA is basically positionless anyway, and a somewhat similar situation has worked out in Philadelphia with James Harden and Tyrese Maxey. Chances are, though, that this one is the most unlikely, in my opinion. Beal seems happy in Washington DC and is getting paid one of the largest contracts in the league. That contract in itself is a bit scary. Can any team even win a championship while paying him that much money?

Conclusion

Both Jaylen Brown and Trae Young are interesting options. Nonetheless, I would still advise the organization to hold off for now. Let’s bring in veterans via free agency and give our young core more time to develop. As exciting as it would be to see them pull off a big trade, chances are the resulting team would still have a ceiling below winning a championship. We’ve all seen what can happen when franchises try and make a huge move before they’re ready; just look at the Nets with Harden and the Clippers with Paul George. I’m not saying that it’s a bad idea in general, but it seems like all of the top teams, regular-season wise, are “home-grown.” For example, Milwaukee, Boston, Denver, and Memphis are all centered around players that were drafted there.
