Why Tyty Washington Deserves an Official Spot in the Houston Rockets’ Rotation

Tyty Washington Jr. may be the point guard of the future for the Houston Rockets. I don’t want to overreact, but his recent performances have been one of the only bright spots for this team. It could be time to look internally within the organization, rather than externally, for a solution to our many problems. Washington was recently recalled to the Rockets’ official roster and should hopefully be given a significant role in the rotation. Given the circumstances, deciding against this option is absolutely not the way to go. There is a real possibility that he could be special in the future, and it would be foolish not to explore that possibility. Below are some reasons why.

Impressive G-league Performance

Probably the most compelling reason why Washington was called up from the Rio Grande Valley Vipers on January 14th in the first place was his outstanding game against the Stockton Kings, where he had a total of 53 points, 10 rebounds, 4 assists, and 2 steals. This certainly didn’t go unnoticed. This brought Washington’s averages to 33.3 points, 6.0 rebounds, 7.3 assists, and 1.7 steals over 3 games with about 38 minutes played per game. Now I know this is the G-league, but those numbers are still incredibly impressive. He should be given a chance to show what he can do against real NBA talent.

Fills a Much Needed Role

The roster has needed a pass-first point guard for a while now, and I think Washington can provide that. He has a unique play style compared to everyone else on the team, and may help fill the void left by Dennis Schroder. See, in the last few years Houston has had a backup primary ball-handler to facilitate the second unit, and this year they don’t. This has been a noticeable struggle throughout the first half of this season. Both Garrison Mathews and Daishen Nix are score-first guards, and are also less important to the future than the former 29th overall pick. I think the minutes should go to Washington over those other guys, and hopefully we’ll see that reflected in the rotation going forward.

Has Confidence

Another thing I want to point out is that Tyty Washington Jr. could bring newfound confidence and energy to the lineup. This is because he hasn’t experienced a majority of this losing streak, which the Rockets are currently on. Having just one guy on the court who believes the team can win makes a huge difference. We also have to remember that he was mocked as a top 20 pick on most draft boards and only fell to number 29 because of some surprising selections. He’s more of a lottery-type talent than a second round pick. There has to be a reason for that, and maybe we just haven't seen it in his very limited number of minutes. All we need is one win to turn everything around, and who better than a point guard to help that cause.

We Need to Try Everything

At this point in the season, it’s time to experiment with everything. Clearly, the current construction isn’t working and some changes need to be made. One of those changes could be giving Washington a significant amount of minutes. This is really the last year where we have the luxury of maintaining so many young players on the roster without being concerned about winning games. Next year, some veterans are going to come in and take over, so we need to be prepared to decide which of our plethora of young prospects is here for the long-term. Washington is one of those prospects who we need to collect data on, which is impossible to do if he isn’t playing.

Final Thoughts

Josh Christopher and Tyty Washington Jr. need to be the only two guards coming off the bench, and I think most Rockets’ fans agree with that statement. Garrison Mathews or Daishen Nix shouldn’t be receiving any playing time if everybody is healthy. The first two names need developmental minutes and it’s a shame that Stephen Silas doesn’t acknowledge that fact. The guard for Kentucky, especially, would help the second unit in more ways than one. It’s time to stop doing the same thing on a game-to-game basis, expecting a different result. Let’s take a step back, adjust what needs to be adjusted, and move from there. If that doesn’t work then repeat that process until it does. Now is not the time for anything to be set in stone.
