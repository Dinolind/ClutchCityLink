4 Houston Rockets Hot Takes For You to Consider Following the End of a Massive Losing Streak

What exactly is a hot take? By definition a hot take is a quickly produced, strongly worded, and often deliberately provocative or sensational opinion or reaction. However, in the sports world it’s basically an unpopular opinion about a certain topic. That’s why sports are so captivating. Every individual has the right to believe what they believe about what has or will take place in the sport that they follow. Not everybody is going to agree on everything, and let’s be honest, that would be incredibly boring if it ever happened. Anyways, below are some of my hot takes regarding the Houston Rockets. If you’re not prepared to disagree then I encourage you to look somewhere else, but if you’re here for something other than the consensus, continue reading.

KPJ Needs To Be Traded Immediately

Kevin Porter Jr. is not the long-term solution at point guard for the Rockets. The organization knows this, I know this, and you probably know this too. He doesn’t mesh well with Jalen Green, and there is bound to be a better replacement through the draft or free agency this summer. That’s why I believe he needs to be moved as soon as the deadline. It probably won’t happen, but his value is peaking right now and we need to be proactive instead of reactive. If a replacement does come along, trading Porter after that would be viewed as something we have to do, giving more leverage to the team acquiring him. The experiment with him at point guard needs to end, and we need to quit while we’re ahead. It’s time to deal the former cavalier for assets now that can help us later get the talent and players we actually need.

Josh Christopher Deserves a Starting Spot

When the roster is fully healthy, Josh Christopher is a benchwarmer. This doesn’t make any sense because it seems like when he plays, good things happen. I know his shooting isn’t great right now; however, I mostly believe that’s due to the limited minutes situation he’s found himself in. There’s just no time for him to get hot, shooting wise, before he gets subbed out. The guard out of Arizona State also consistently posts a positive plus-minus, which is saying something considering Houston is the worst team in the league, and he can score on basically anyone if given the opportunity. He would be the perfect replacement if the above trade were to happen. Not to mention that Jalen Green and Christopher have been friends way before the NBA and probably have some on-court chemistry already.

Sengun Isn’t That Good Yet

Sorry to bring you back down to earth, but Alperen Sengun isn’t some savior who can lead the team to wins right now. His offense is spectacular in moments, yes, but he isn’t aggressive enough or consistent enough to run everything through him. Anything Alpi does offer on offense is pretty much canceled out by how bad his defense is. He also can’t stay out of foul trouble. Although I like Sengun and think he’s a decent player, he’s nowhere near to what the casual fan is led to believe by the mainstream media recently. The comparisons he draws to Nikola Jokic are disrespectful to how good the reigning back-to-back MVP is, and that’s coming from a Rockets’ fan. The Turkish League star needs to develop a lot more before he’s even considered an above average big man. I think he just stands out because he’s been the highlight man on a bad team these past few weeks.

Jabari Smith Jr. Should Be the #2 Option

This one could be the least egregious opinion, yet I’m surprised it’s not talked about more. Why is our number 3 overall pick like the 5th option on a rebuilding team? It doesn’t make any sense. We should be doing what the Pistons were doing before Cade Cunningham got injured, by prioritizing first, our high 2021 pick and then second, our high 2022 pick. Jabari should be treated the same way Jalen was last year, except for that Jalen should still be ahead of him in the pecking order. The other starters need minutes, too, but Smith is supposed to be one of the foundational pieces to Houston’s future success. It pains me to watch him become essentially a guy that stands in the corner or on the wing, when he can do so much more. Would it really cause that many issues to experiment with that idea once he comes back into the lineup?

Final Thoughts

The Rockets are still in a bad place overall, even after winning their first game in 2023 against the Minnesota Timberwolves on January 23rd. It was nice to finally outscore the other team, but that doesn’t make up for all the problems which have been growing over the past few months. At this point this season feels like a lost cause, though, I really hope it doesn’t end up that way. There’s no excuse anymore for how this team can be this bad with the talent it has. I know I’ve said this a lot, but changes have to be made. Everybody deserves blame and should be held responsible for what they can control. That’s just what I believe. Feel free to differ. I don’t even really know where I stand anymore on the question of continuing to rebuild. The losing has just been awful to watch, and I’m just hoping it’ll all be worth it someday.
