Frank Kaminsky Player Profile and Potential Future Role With the Rockets

Frank Kaminsky is now the only person left from the Rockets’ trade deadline return after John Wall, Justin Holiday, and Danny Green have all been waived. That means it’s time to figure out what his future place on this team looks like. He’s relatively unknown as a player also, so I wanted to bring to light some of his strengths and weaknesses on the court. He’s not going to be a franchise-altering player by any means, but it’s still important to make sure that he’s integrated properly. Where exactly does his skillset lie and how will he fit into this already overcrowded rotation? Continue reading for my best analysis of those questions given the current facts.

Player Profile

Frank Kaminsky, acquired from the Atlanta Hawks, is a 7’ 0” tall power forward who also has the ability to run some small ball 5. That’s not because of his size, but more of his skillset. He specializes in 3-point shooting. In fact, the former national college player of the year shot nearly 48% from 3 this season with the Hawks before the transaction. That’s obviously an outlier considering his career percentage is closer to 35%, but it’s still incredibly impressive over a decent size stretch of 26 games. On this roster he instantly becomes one of our best shooters, which is a bit sad honestly for us, though, encouraging for him.

Another thing I noticed was his ability to position himself properly. Kaminsky is not going to take guys off the dribble or be a shot creator by any stretch of the imagination, but he definitely knows when and how to get to certain spots. His ability to cut when the shot clock starts winding down shouldn’t be overlooked. He also can seemingly get himself open by exploiting double teams on other players or simply using the traditional pick-and-pop strategy. That being said, the 9th overall pick in the 2015 draft isn’t exactly a good interior presence. He thrives on the perimeter, and for that reason Kaminsky appears to perform much better with another “true” big man alongside him.

Future Role

In order to accurately gauge Frank Kaminsky's role, we first have to figure out which guys he’s in competition with. Considering he’s a 4 or 5 generally we have to look at the bench players for that position. This is because Alperen Sengun and Jabari Smith Jr. are more than likely going to be starters for the foreseeable future. In this case, Usman Garuba, Tari Eason, and possibly Darius Days are the people who could fill those spots off the bench as well as Kaminsky himself. Realistically, 3 of those guys and going to play is one is not. Right now, I believe that decision comes down to Days and Kaminsky. Both Garuba and Eason have more value to the organization, and we definitely need to see them on the court for the last stretch of this season.

I think right now Kaminsky wins out on that third spot. Days has shown flashes, but he’s still mainly a g-league player for the time being. This all basically means that Kaminsky will likely receive about 10 minutes a game during the rest of the season in Houston. Funny enough, that’s actually the exact number he’s played throughout the first 2 times we’ve seen him on the court since the trade happened. My only fear is that Stephen Silas will lean on him too much seeing as he is truly the only veteran on the roster who will get rotation minutes, sorry Boban. We’ve seen his tendency to go to Eric Gordon in close moments, and I just hope that doesn’t happen again with someone who’s on an expiring contract and not majorly important to the future of this team.

Conclusion

Frank the Tank, as his nickname suggests, could bring new firepower to this lineup if inserted properly. He brings shooting, which is nice and something we desperately need, but I just hope he’s not overused. Kaminsky is likely a one-year rental anyways because once his contract is up at the end of this season, I doubt either side will be interested in continuing together any further. I could be wrong, though, and that’s the beauty of the NBA. Things can change overnight. This isn’t a blockbuster addition, but that doesn’t mean it’s not worth paying attention to. Every little thing matters when trying to become a contender after fully committing to a rebuild. A circumstance that the Rockets currently find themselves in.
