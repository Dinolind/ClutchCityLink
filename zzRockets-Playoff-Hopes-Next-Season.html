Can the Houston Rockets Realisitcally Make the Playoffs in the 2023-2024 Season?

The NBA playoffs are right around the corner, and unfortunately, the Houston Rockets are nowhere near one of the coveted 20 spots. However, hope exists among many suggesting there may be a path forward that can turn this team around as soon as next year. While the odds say otherwise, there are plenty of reasons to think that it could legitimately be possible. If you want a more full breakdown of these reasons, I suggest checking out this article before continuing. With that out of the way, I’ll start with the teams that are almost guaranteed to be above Houston next season, barring any major unforeseen circumstances:

Denver Nuggets

Memphis Grizzlies

Phoenix Suns

Sacramento Kings

Golden State Warriors

New Orleans Pelicans

OKC Thunder

Then, we have the teams that are, most likely, going to be towards the bottom of the conference: Utah Jazz, Portland Trail Blazers, San Antonio Spurs. These organizations simply are not ready to make that leap towards the NBA playoffs. That leaves the Dallas Mavericks, Minnesota Timberwolves, Los Angeles Lakers, Los Angeles Clippers, and the Rockets themselves left, with only 3 play-in spots up for grabs. Below is a breakdown of each of those teams along with my predictions for whether or not Houston will be ranked above or below them for the 2023-2024 season.

Dallas Mavericks

In short, the pairing of Kyrie Irving and Luka Doncic hasn’t worked out so far. With Irving’s free agency looming this summer, the Mavericks could be in real trouble. Currently, they are the 11th seed in the Western Conference. What happens when they, potentially, lose an all-star starter? I know Doncic is one of the best players in the NBA, but he can’t win games all by himself. The role players Dallas sent to the Nets for Irving were more valuable to their roster than people thought. Although it may be a bit of a shocker, I’m taking Houston here. There is just too much uncertainty going on with the Mavs. In contrast, we are likely to have one of the deepest rosters in the league next year, and it’ll be basically the opposite for them.

Minnesota Timberwolves

Minnesota is home to another star pairing that hasn’t worked out very well this season: Rudy Gobert and Karl-Anthony Towns. Their offense has been terrible, and it's not like there’s really any room for improvement. Anthony Edwards is good, but I just think our roster will be better. Of course, this is all very dependent on how this off-season goes. If everything follows the plan, though, I could see the Rockets outplaying Minnesota over the course of next season. Despite the T-Wolves being in the play-in at the present moment, they actually have a negative point differential. By that metric, they should be closer to the 11th seed. KAT has been injured for most of the year, yet I still don’t feel confident in their chances going forward. They’re a borderline play-in team, at most, for the foreseeable future.

Los Angeles Lakers

It feels like the Lakers are up and down every other week. After having an abysmal start to the year, they have managed to crawl their way back to the 7th seed. I’m going to put the Lakers above Houston for one simple reason: they have to be good next season. Lebron James is nearing 40 years old, and the front office did not trade away that many first round picks to waste the last few years of his career. Anthony Davis has also been stepping up recently. If this roster can stay healthy, they are easily one of the best in the league. Plus, there are also numerous rumors of them preparing to sign Kyrie Irving this off-season. The Lakers always find a way to make the playoffs, and our young roster just doesn’t have the experience to really compete with them.

Los Angeles Clippers

The Clippers are another interesting situation. If they come up short yet again this postseason, there is a legitimate chance they will decide to “blow it up.” Kawhi Leonard can’t stay healthy and is already 31, Paul George is 32. Even some of their role players are getting up there in age as well. The GM may just decide it’s not worth keeping this roster together anymore, and extracting as much value as he can for his assets on the open market. Both star players can also choose to decline their player options for 2024-2025, making them free agents after next season. That would be scary as an owner, especially if this team doesn’t even make the playoffs. The Clipper curse is real, but, if this group stays together, I still have to put them above the Rockets. They just have way too much talent for me to simply ignore.

Final Thoughts

I think the Houston Rockets will finish somewhere around the 9th or 10th seed in the Western Conference and get bounced by a more experienced team in the play-in tournament. They’re just still a long way off, and haven't made very much improvement, team-wise, this season compared to last. It’s rare to witness a team jump from the bottom of the league immediately into the playoffs, but I can see it happening. The front office is under immense pressure to put, at the very least, a watchable product on the court by October. This fanbase will simply refuse to go along with the rebuild any longer, as even our pick will be in the hands of OKC. The sooner we can get to the off-season the better. Big changes are coming.
