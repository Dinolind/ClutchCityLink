3 Trade Targets the Rockets Should Consider At the 2023 NBA Deadline

The NBA trade season is unofficially upon us as teams prepare to construct their final roster for this season by February 9th, excluding buyout signings of course. Now that I’ve vetoed the idea of trading for John Collins, I wanted to propose some other options that make more sense, in my opinion. We all know who’s on the trade block for the Rockets by now: Eric Gordon, Kenyon Martin Jr., and possibly Kevin Porter Jr., depending on how he’s currently viewed by the organization. I don’t expect all of these guys to be traded, but I think it’s at least worth exploring the possibilities of what could be out there for the Rockets. Spoiler alert, there aren’t going to be any mock trades, just a list of a few players that Houston may want to inquire about and possibly trade for.

Doug McDermott

Doug McDermott is a 6 ’7 ” small forward who plays for the Spurs. San Antonio is likely to be sellers this trade deadline with what little pieces they have left from the Dejounte-Murray era. One of these pieces is McDermott, and I think he would be a great fit for the Rockets. His specialty for his whole career has been his ability to shoot the 3-ball, which is something Houston desperately needs. In fact, McDermott is currently averaging 41.5% from beyond the arc on over 4 attempts per game. Think like Garrison Mathews plus. Anyways, he would fill the role of Eric Gordon, to a lesser extent minutes-wise, while providing another veteran presence.

The former National College Player of the Year is currently on a 3-year/$41,250,000 contract, which is not outrageously expensive for a quality role player that can shoot. I also can’t imagine his trade value being very high. Maybe just one of our lower tier young players like Tyty Washington or Usman Garuba, or maybe the 2023 Bucks pick that we have, would be enough for the Spurs to say yes. They need to restock their assets and we need more reliable shot makers, seems like a win-win to me. We’re going to need players like him eventually anyways, so why not go after them now. I view it as a low risk, high reward situation because this team would definitely benefit tremendously from some more floor spacing.

Richaun Holmes

Richaun Holmes is a 6 '8 " center that plays for the Kings. He has become expendable due to the recent blossoming of Domantas Sabonis. They both occupy relatively the same role, so Holmes has been put on the trade block. The Rockets could really use a guy like him, and again, it wouldn’t cost them all that much. I think Usman Garuba works better at power forward, so I could see Holmes coming off the bench at the 5 spot or potentially starting on some nights. I think the package should include Bruno Fernando and some kind of asset, to make the deal go through. The Rockets need vets to steady this young roster, and Holmes could be one of those people.

He’s also not on a bad contract either, 4 years/$46,522,569, which expires after the 2024-2025 season. While a backup should probably be a bit cheaper, I’m not against Holmes starting, alongside Sengun, for the time being. The biggest selling point, for me at least, is that he’s shooting nearly 70% from the field. That’s incredibly impressive and would help out both KPJ and Jalen Green, who like to run the pick and roll. We struck out last summer on acquiring a new big-man, so now is the time to make a move. We can also flip Holmes this off-season if things don’t work out, so I don’t really see any reason not to. We have too many young players as it is, so it makes sense to get rid of 1 or 2 of them for a veteran that can actually help this team get back on the right track.

OG Anunoby

Obviously this one is extremely unlikely to actually occur, but I wanted to include 1 guy that was more than just a role player. At least, make a case for one, I guess. Anunoby is currently on a Raptors team that is likely to sell most of their roster this deadline. There are several teams linked to him, and I think the Rockets should be one of them. First off, he’s only 25 years old, so the timeline shouldn’t really be an issue. And he fits exactly what Houston struggles with the most: defense, shooting, and shot-creation. Just for a minute imagine a starting lineup of Scoot Henderson, Jalen Green, Anunoby, Jabari Smith Jr., and Alperen Sengun. Something like that isn’t completely out of the realm of possibility.

I know that the trade package would be a lot, but I think Rafael Stone has the assets if he really wanted to be the highest bidder. The main piece would be Eric Gordon, and then we would essentially attach valuable assets around him. Right now, it seems like 3 unprotected picks is his market value, which we definitely have. He’s under contract for at least next season and then has a player option in 2024, which he will likely decline. This means we need to secure some kind of agreement where he would sign an extension after being traded. I think if the Rockets want an upper-echelon player, he’s the guy to go for. There’s not going to be many others who are available, young, and fit the roster.

Final Thoughts

No one knows what’s going to happen, and that’s the beauty of the final few minutes before the transaction period ends. Maybe the Rockets will do absolutely nothing and save their cards for the offseason, which is more likely in my opinion, anyways. I really just hope Eric Gordon finds a new team. He’s been here for way longer than he should’ve been, and clearly both sides need to move on from each other. Other than that, I don’t really expect much else. The team has chosen to tank, yet again, and the current roster is doing just fine at that, so there’s no reason, from the GM’s perspective, to do anything substantial. We probably just have to wait a few more months before things really start to happen.
