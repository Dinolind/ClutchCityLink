Rockets vs Pistons vs Spurs: An Update On the Race to the Bottom In 2023

The NBA season is winding down, and with only a few games left, it’s time to revisit the draft lottery landscape. With prized possession Victor Wembanyama up for grabs, these final few matchups could really make a difference. It has recently been confirmed that the bottom three teams will, indeed, be the Houston Rockets, Detroit Pistons, and San Antonio Spurs. All that’s left to determine now is the order. Currently, Houston has a record of 18-59, San Antonio at 19-57, and Detroit at 16-60. What follows is a complete breakdown of each team’s remaining games as well as my predictions for the official lottery odds order.

Houston Rockets

There are only 5 games left on the schedule that need to be considered here. I feel confident in saying the Rockets will comfortably lose to the Lakers, Nuggets, and Wizards on 4/2, 4/4, and 4/9 respectively. The only 2 games I could see going either way are 3/31 against the Pistons and 4/7 against the Hornets. The former is particularly interesting because it involves two of the teams competing for the worst record. This means someone is going to have to win the game. My gut is telling me that it will be the Rockets. Stephen Silas has repeatedly shown to be unwilling to lose games on purpose by not playing certain players. Apparently, his resume is more important than the future of our franchise, at this point. Something is telling me we will go 2-3, finishing with a record of 20-62. One win better than last season, putting them in 2nd place out of the bunch.

Detroit Pistons

The Pistons have 6 games left. They are more than likely going to lose against the Heat on 4/4, the Nets on 4/5, the Pacers on 4/7, and, lastly, against the Bulls on 4/9. That leaves two games, the one mentioned before against the Rockets and the one against Orlando on 4/2. Orlando is past the point of competing for anything, and may go all out to secure the 3rd worst record in the Eastern Conference. However, Detroit is just not going to end the season on a 13-game losing streak. I’m predicting them with at least 1, if not 2, more victories before the final buzzer of game 82. Nonetheless, I don’t see how they won’t end up with the worst record. The Spurs are currently 3 wins ahead, and the Rockets will be too if that particular game goes how I’m expecting. I’m predicting their record at 18-64.

San Antonio Spurs

The Spurs are in somewhat of a different situation. Every single one of their remaining 6 games is against opposing organizations actively trying to win, except maybe their game on 4/6 vs the Portland Trail Blazers. Anything can happen in the NBA, so I’m going to say one more win for them left in the year. Greg Popovich has too much respect and pride to have a double-digit losing streak heading into the off-season. That puts their final record at 20-62, just like the Rockets. This would result in a 50/50 chance between the two teams for the 2nd best lottery odds. A coin flip, if you will. I’m not sure if the winner of this flip will be ever be revealed, but it certainly matters as to whether the teams’ pick can fall to #6 or #7.

Final Thoughts

For all we know, the lottery is completely rigged, and the NBA has already decided the order. However, I just think something would’ve come out by now from an anonymous insider. Everything points to the fact that the Rockets should aim to lose their remaining games, yet, for whatever reason, I just don’t envision that happening. If you’re going to tune into one more game this season, make it the one against the Pistons on March 31st. Either you will see, likely, the final win of the season, or Houston coming one step closer to passing Detroit for seed number 30. It’s a shame we as fans feel obligated to root against our own team, but that’s just the way most major sports work. We all saw what happens when a team wins meaningless games at the end of the season, with the Texans, so, hopefully, the Rockets won’t make that same mistake.
