The Rockets Have the Fourth Overall Pick: What Happens Now?

In case you have yet to see the results of the draft lottery by now, the Rockets landed at 4th behind the Trailblazers at 3, the Hornets at 2, and the Spurs at number 1. While this result led to an immediate reaction of anger and disappointment for most, there are still plenty of options lying ahead. It definitely wasn’t the best case scenario, but it’s not the worst either. At least with the lottery out of the way, the organization can take the cards they have been dealt and continue with the best possible move. The most pressing issue currently is to figure out what to do with our draft pick. Below are some options to potentially look into.

Draft With It

The easiest answer is to simply draft with the pick. However, that decision comes with its own set of questions and concerns. As it stands today, the top three picks, in order, are likely to be Victor Wembanyama, Scoot Henderson, and Brandon Miller. That leaves Houston, per the general consensus, with the following choices: Amen Thompson, Ausar Thompson, Cam Whitmore, and Jarace Walker. However, they are probably eyeing one of the Thompson brothers, specifically Amen. I’m not any kind of scout or draft expert, so take the following with a grain of salt. Amen Thompson is a 6’7” point guard that excels in playmaking, defense, and getting downhill, but struggles with hitting shots outside of the paint. The closest NBA comp we have is Ben Simmons with slightly better shooting.

The other twin, Ausar Thompson, is a better shooter, but he isn’t nearly as aggressive or quite the same ball-handler and playmaker as his brother. That being said, he does still have the same solid frame and athleticism. Personally, I have Ausar over Amen for the simple reason that our team doesn’t need another player who can’t shoot. We’ve seen Ausar develop his jump shot dramatically over this past season, and the same can’t be said for Amen. Amen has a higher ceiling if he can start making shots, but we all said the same thing about Ben Simmons and look how that turned out. Cam Whitmore and Jarace Walker are both intriguing options as well, though I doubt it would make sense to draft either one of those guys at 4.

Trade Options

This category has three subcategories: trade up, trade down, or trade out. The first scenario, in which they seek to acquire a better pick, would almost certainly involve the Charlotte Hornets or Portland Trail Blazers, with the prospect in mind being Scoot Henderson. Although Brandon Miller is interesting as well, many believe we already have a player possessing a similar skill set and role with Jabari Smith Jr. Henderson, on the other hand, gives this roster a much-needed point guard that can develop alongside the rest of our young core. However, this begs the question about what it would take to trade up into the top 3 and secure him on draft night. While there are many varying opinions on the matter, basically everyone agrees that it won’t come cheap.

As for trading down, a lot more possibilities open up. Anthony Black, Gradey Dick, Taylor Hendricks, and Jarace Walker have all been proposed as options for the Rockets in the 7-10 range. Any one of these guys would come off the bench and be more of a complimentary piece rather than part of the core group. However, we may gain another role player or future draft pick(s) in exchange for moving back in the order. Then there’s the third option of trading the pick entirely without getting another pick for the same draft in return. Doing so would get us considerably more on the open trade market from any team looking to start a rebuilding phase. That being said, we would also lose another shot at landing and developing a future superstar.

Final Thoughts

Being at #4 poses a lot of interesting questions. At 1 or 2, the answer was obvious: draft Victor Wembanyama or Scoot Henderson respectively. At 5 or 6, the pick was all but guaranteed to get traded. Being in the middle, the organization can go either way. I can’t really rule out any possibilities currently, but I am leaning towards them simply just drafting Amen Thompson or trading up for Scoot Henderson. The other options seem a bit too radical and open-ended to foresee anything happening between now and June 22nd. It will be interesting to watch, though, because most major insiders and analysts are predicting some movement within the top 4. For now, it’s too early to call as much more is yet to be discussed.
