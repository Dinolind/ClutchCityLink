Draft Lottery Scenarios: What Happens Depending On Where the Rockets Land?

We are now less than a month away from the NBA draft lottery. Where the Rockets ultimately end up will have drastic implications on this team moving forward. After winning the coin toss for the second best odds, Houston can no longer fall to pick number 7. Their chances of landing at each spot are as follows (rounded to the nearest tenth): 1-14%, 2-13.4%, 3-12.7%, 4-12%, 5-27.8%, 6-20%. That begs the question about what to do with that pick once we know what number it’s at. Luckily, we do have about a month to figure that out between the lottery and the actual draft, but it doesn’t hurt anything to start discussing that topic now.

Picks 1-3

If the Rockets land a top 3 pick in this year’s draft, chances are they’ll end up drafting with it. The consensus big board at the time of writing is Victor Wembanyama at number 1, Scoot Henderson at number 2, and Brandon Miller at number 3. While there has been some talk recently of the Rockets potentially selecting Miller over Henderson, I doubt this will actually happen due to the fact that Henderson is just too good of a prospect to pass up on. Regardless, my point still remains that, provided we land a top 3 pick, one of these prospects will be on the team heading into next season. That is unless some team is willing to offer an outrageous trade package for Wemby, which I don’t think any of them will have the guts to do.

However, these guys' roles on the roster may still be up for debate. I think Wembanyama starts at the 4 or 5 no matter what, but what does that mean for Jabari Smith Jr. or Alperen Sengun? In Henderson’s case, he would potentially move Kevin Porter Jr. to the bench, which we all know would not likely not go over very well. The alternative is to have Henderson come off the bench, but I doubt that most fans would agree with that decision. Then there’s the issue of what happens if James Harden returns. While I’d imagine that his return doesn’t happen with Henderson on the roster, who really knows? With Brandon Miller, I think it’s a bit easier. He would slide in at the 3, moving KJ Martin back to the bench where he’s been most effective in my opinion.

Picks 4-6

After pick number 3, things get a bit more complicated. There seems to be 4 prospects that are likely to be selected in the 4-6 range: Amen Thompson, Ausar Thompson, Jarace Walker, and Cam Whitmore. However, I would like to see the Rockets actually trade this pick if it falls outside of the top 3. We already have two high level prospects to develop, and I don’t think we need another unless that prospect is a generational talent. It’s not worth using a top 6 pick in a loaded draft class for somebody that isn’t going to have a very good opportunity at succeeding with this team. There will be too many people ahead of that person on the roster, so they’ll be the 7th or 8th man in the rotation at best. By that point, it’s better to just trade the pick and get someone who will make more of an impact next season.

Some star level names that have already come up are Trae Young, Jaylen Brown, and Bradley Beal. I’ve already discussed each of these players in my previous article, so there’s no sense reiterating those statements here. There’s also the possibility of flipping this pick for 2 or more future firsts down the line. This opens up flexibility when dealing with future trades while not making a commitment to any one guy currently available. Houston actually did the reverse of this, by giving up two picks, for Alperen Sengun a couple years back. Regardless, an established player is going to be worth a lot more than a pick in the 4-6 range at this point in our rebuild.

Final Thoughts

If we can walk out of this lottery scenario with either a top-3 pick or a quality veteran, I think a majority of people should be satisfied. It really comes down to a 40.2% chance one way and a 59.8% chance the other way. That basically means a 3/5 chance exists where we don’t land Wembanyama, Henderson, or Miller. All I’m saying is, don’t think it’s a guarantee. We could always trade up to one of those picks, but the amount of assets it would require is likely not going to be worth it. Everything I just discussed is why the draft lottery matters so much. It can either give us a pathway towards contention in the near future, or it can lead to yet another season focused on development. We’ll just have to wait and see how everything plays out.
