Teams To Watch As a Rockets’ Fan For the Final Few Games of the Season

It won’t be long before the NBA playoffs officially begin, but until then there are still a few important games left to be played. Unfortunately, the Rockets have long been eliminated from contention. However, that doesn’t mean these games don’t matter to us. Especially in the West, where there is only a 2 game difference between the 5th and 9th seed. The bottom 4 and 4 are decided, yet there is still the possibility for a lot of movement in the middle. In the East, the final non-play-in spot is still up for grabs, and the 8th and 9th seeds could easily flip as well. Below are some teams, as a Rockets fan, that you should be keeping a close eye on as the season wraps up.

Nets

The Brooklyn Nets are one of the two teams fighting for the 6th seed out East. The reason their games matter to us is because Houston holds the right to swap its pick this season for Brooklyn’s. In an ideal scenario, the Nets would fall to the 7th seed and get bounced from the play-in tournament in just two games, leading to their spot in the draft lottery. As a result, most likely the 14th best odds for the top pick would be headed their way. This positioning equates to a 0.5% chance at #1, 0.6% chance at #2, 0.6% chance at #3, and 0.7% chance at number 4. This isn’t much, but would give Houston the best odds out of anyone to land the first, second, third, or fourth picks when you add their own odds into the mix as well. We need every chance we can get to land a top prospect, so having Brooklyn lose their final 2 games against the Orlando Magic tonight and the 76ers on Sunday is crucial.

Heat

The Miami Heat are the team that could knock down the Nets into the play-in. Their record is currently 43-37 with 2 games left in the season, while only being 1 game back from Brooklyn. Those two games are against the Wizards tonight and the Magic on Sunday. Both of those teams have already been eliminated from playoff contention, and should, hopefully, be easy wins. The Nets do hold the tiebreaker, so everything has to go perfectly. If this turns out to be the case, the Heat would finish at 45-37 while the Nets would crumble and finish at 44-36. It’s a small chance, but worth mentioning. I think it really comes down to the Brooklyn vs Orlando game tonight at 6:30pm CST. Chances are the Heat beat both of their opponents, and that the Netswill lose to the 76ers. As a result, this game will determine it. If a miracle happens and Brooklyn lands the first or second pick, eventually heading our way, we can thank Miami.

Spurs

Whether or not San Antonio wins or loses its final 2 games could determine if the Rockets can fall to pick 6 or all the way to pick 7 in the upcoming lottery on May 16th. The Spurs are currently 1 game ahead of the Rockets in the standings. San Antonio plays the Timberwolves tomorrow and the Mavericks on Sunday. Even if they lose both of these games, which is somewhat likely, the Rockets still have a chance to claim the 2nd best odds. Houston, on the other hand, plays the Hornets today and the Wizards on Sunday. These two teams also have nothing to play for and will likely try to lose. It really comes down to who can be the worst. If the Spurs can win one of their games, then we have a little bit more wiggle room to win one of ours. We shouldn’t take any chances of falling to pick #7, even at just a 7.1% chance. If the Spurs can just win one of these games, they would remain above us even if we beat the Hornets.

Clippers

The last team to mention is the Los Angeles Clippers and all 3 of the teams below them that have a chance to pass them up. See, the Clippers gave us the rights to swap our 2023 Buck’s pick for their own during the Eric Gordon trade. If the Clippers fall into the play-in, they could even end up in the Lottery. Now, the pick is top-6 protected, so there’s no chance of it ending up in the top 4. Regardless, I’d still much prefer, say the 13th pick, as opposed to somewhere in the 20s. The Clippers are playing the Trail Blazers tomorrow and the Suns on Sunday. They will probably win the first of those two games and lose the second. This means we need two of the following three teams to win their last two games: the Warriors, Lakers, and Pelicans. Having a mid-teens pick as opposed to a later one in the order can be very valuable in trades as well. The Clippers falling in the standings could be the missing piece to a big off-season transaction.

Final Thoughts

As you can see, there are still many implications for the remaining NBA games concerning the Rockets. In short, we want the Clippers to lose, the Spurs to win, the Nets to lose, and the Heat to win. If those 4 things happen, then our lottery odds could marginally improve. For the Rockets themselves, we need to lose these final 2 games. It isn’t worth winning one meaningless game in April to fall one more spot in the draft. The race against the Pistons is over, but the one against the Spurs is still on. This entire season and all the suffering is going to be rewarded on lottery night. Just imagine the celebration if the Rockets do land a top 2 pick. The future of the franchise is largely dependent on what happens this summer, and these remaining games could change that greatly. I can’t wait for the playoffs to begin.
