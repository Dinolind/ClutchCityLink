Houston Rockets Free Agency Review: Talk About A Wild Offseason

Wow. Just taking a step back and realizing the sheer number of moves made in such a short period of time has sent shockwaves across the entire fanbase. The 2023 free agency period was everything it was hyped up to be from Houston’s perspective, if not more. That being said, there were certainly a handful of questionable decisions as well. There’s plenty to discuss, and we have ample time to do so. It’s just so rare to see this level of roster turnover in the NBA, let alone for a rebuilding team that doesn’t have immediate championship-level expectations or a star player influencing the front office. I still think there’s more to come, but so far, this is where Houston stands.

Who Stayed and Who’s Gone?

The one thing Houston did was keep the “core 6”,composed of their higher-tier draft picks from the last few seasons, fully intact. These players, of course, are: Jalen Green, Alperen Sengun, Jabari Smith Jr., Tari Eason, Amen Thompson, and Cam Whitmore. So far, the only other two players who’ve remained with the team are Kevin Porter Jr. and, surprisingly, Jae’Sean Tate. That means, Kenyon Martin Jr., Josh Christopher, Usman Garuba, Tyty Washington Jr., and Daishen Nix are no longer with the organization. The player I’ll miss the most is Christopher. It felt like he always played well when he got a chance, and just had his once-in-a-lifetime NBA opportunity ruined by Stephen Silas. I’m telling you, he’s going to flourish in Memphis if given the proper role.

So what exactly happened? As I mentioned, Christopher was traded to the Grizzlies as part of a sign-and-trade for Dillon Brooks. Garuba and Tyty Washington Jr. were sent to the Atlanta Hawks, along with 2 second round picks (Houston 2028, Minnesota 2025), for the draft rights to Alpha Kaba. Kaba is a 27 year old, 6’10” power forward/center who was drafted in 2017 with the 60th overall pick, and he’s yet to play a single NBA minute. Lastly, Kenyon Martin Jr. was traded to the Los Angeles Clippers for 2 future seconds and Daishen Nix was waived. Out of these 3 trades, none of them look very enticing at first glance. I think they were centered around freeing up cap space to sign more veteran free agents, but it really sucks to see so many of our young guys leave for underwhelming returns.

Additions

The headlining signing that basically has been everywhere is that Houston gave Fred VanVleet from the Toronto Raptors a 3-year contract worth 130 million dollars. This sounds like a lot of money, because it is. In fact, it’s the highest annual value that he was eligible for. However, the third year is actually a team option. VanVleet is only guaranteed the first $85 million over 2 seasons. After that, the front office has the power to decide what happens next. If Amen Thompson is ready sooner than expected, he can have the starting spot sooner and vice versa. We had to spend the money somewhere, and this was a worthwhile investment. His personality, leadership skills, and on-court abilities are exactly what this team needs. He is going to impact everything positively in so many different ways.

Other than VanVleet, Houston added 3 other key veteran role players: Dillon Brooks, Jock Landale, and Jeff Green. Brooks signed first for 4 years/$80 million. This is a ridiculous number, but I can almost say for certain that it is not all guaranteed money. Whether it be a team option for the final year, or some sort of incentive-based rewards, I believe we’re missing some details at this point in time. For Landale, we know his deal is worth 4 years/$32 million and that only the first year is confirmed. He’s on a large-scale “prove-it” deal, which is unique for a guy of his caliber. Jeff Green is on a cheaper 1-year contract, $6 million to be exact, and should provide some veteran mentorship to young players and a few spot minutes here and there.

What Next?

One last thing to mention is that Houston acquired Patty Mills from the Brooklyn Nets for an undisclosed return, with Mills being included in the sign-and-trade for Dillion Brooks. This move may give the Rockets a bit more cap space to maneuver with, but we’ll have to wait and see. I also think Jae’Sean Tate will be on the move soon. With signing Dillon Brooks, drafting Cam Whitmore, and still having Tari Eason and Jabari Smith Jr. on the roster, Tate appears to be the odd man out. With everyone else getting dealt, I don’t see a world where he remains on the roster unless no other team offers anything of value between now and the start of the season. Everything that just happened is going to take a while to process, and the truth is, we likely won’t be able to judge the moves made until we get to the start of the season in October.
