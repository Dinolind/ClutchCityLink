Recap and Analysis of the Houston Rockets' Trade Deadline Moves

The 2023 NBA trade deadline has come and gone, and it ended up being a lot bigger and exciting than originally expected. I’ve already covered the main impacts of the Nets’ trades, as it pertains to the Rockets here, so I’ll be focusing more on the moves Houston actually made themselves. In total there were two, with the 3 players traded being Eric Gordon, Garrison Mathews, and Bruno Fernando. Although I have to admit it was a little bit underwhelming, we should still be happy that anything was done at all. I’m just glad we get to move on with a more cohesive roster than what we had before, and that nothing majorly confusing occurred. Anyways, here’s a breakdown of everything that happened leading up to 2:00pm CST on February 9th.

Trade #1

Rockets receive: Justin Holiday, Frank Kaminsky, 2024 2nd round pick via OKC from Atlanta, 2025 2nd round pick via OKC from Atlanta

Hawks receive: Garrison Mathwes, Bruno Fernando

My immediate reaction to this transaction was that there was nothing to get overly excited about. The reality is, though, Garrison Mathews and Bruno Fernando no longer had a place on this team. Both guys have been in and out of the rotation, and everybody knew that they weren’t going to be here by the end of the rebuild. Their positions are simply too oversaturated with talent on this roster. Fernando being gone, in particular, opens up minutes for Usman Garuba. Garuba should’ve been getting these minutes anyways, but now there’s no other choice, which is nice. Josh Christopher and Tyty Washington Jr. should also receive more playing time in Mathews’ absence as well.

The Hawks did this trade simply for monetary reasons. Holiday is owed over $6,000,000 this season, which prompted Atlanta to give up some second round picks to get off that money. I also like, for the Rockets, that both Kaminsky and Holiday are on expiring contracts, so there’s no pressure to give them a roster spot after this year. Not to mention, more expiring contracts generally lead to more cap space in free agency. The most we’ll get out of either guy is probably some spot minutes off the bench; that is if Houston even has any intention of playing either one, which I assume they will. Also, the picks are nice to have, but they likely won’t be very good. OKC projects to improve dramatically over the next few seasons and will fall a few spots in the second round as a result, by 2024. However, the picks are probably going to be thrown in some future deal anyways as opposed to Houston actually using them.

Trade #2

Rockets receive: John Wall, Danny Green, Right to swap 2023 Bucks first round pick with 2023 Clippers first round pick (Top 6 protected)

Grizzlies receive: Luke Kennard

Clippers receive: 3 second round picks via Memphis, Eric Gordon

The move finally happened. Eric Gordon is now finally no longer a Houston Rocket, a statement which has been long overdue. There are now 0 players remaining from the “James-Harden Era” and we can continue as fans to look towards the future. I think the return is average at best, but at least it’s over and done with. John Wall is obviously going to be waived immediately; there’s simply no way he comes back to this organization after his negative comments made towards them. Then, Danny Green is really the main piece. He’s supposedly a 3-and-D wing that’s unfortunately past his prime, but I think he can still have a positive influence on our group of young guys. People connected to Memphis have raved about his locker-room presence, which is something we desperately need, especially with Eric Gordon now gone.

The last piece we got back is the right to swap our Bucks pick with the Clippers pick for this season. While I’m not opposed to this, it doesn’t seem like that big of a deal. The Clippers are still a contender, according to most of the league, so having the ability to swap for their pick may only move it up like 5 spots. Still, though, it is yet another asset for the front office to play around with this off-season, which is definitely a plus. There’s also the off chance where the Clippers don’t make the playoffs, making the pick even better, though that’s unlikely considering what we know about their future. The thing I’m most anticipating, however, is what will happen to the minutes that have been reserved for Gordon? KJ Martin, Tari Eason, and Jae’Sean Tate will all, almost certainly, be given more opportunity, so it’ll be interesting to see how those guys handle it.

Final Thoughts

If someone asked me to grade the deadline for the Rockets, I would give them a B. The trades the front office made were fine, not outstanding by any means, and they did exactly what we as fans expected them to. No flashy transactions involving Kevin Porter Jr., Jae'Sean Tate, or any other core pieces, but nothing disappointing either. The NBA landscape has completely changed, and it’s going to be incredibly fun to watch it all play out over these next few months. The storylines are enormous, which will make this year’s playoffs one to remember. It’s funny, knowing what we know now, how this trade season was supposed to be underwhelming, and it turned out to be arguably the biggest one in history. The bottom line is that the Rockets did what they needed to do, and it’s fine that something bigger didn’t happen because it’s just not the right year for their timeline. That being said, I would encourage you to stay on high alert this summer because I know Rafael Stone is just waiting to make the perfect, big trade at the right time.
