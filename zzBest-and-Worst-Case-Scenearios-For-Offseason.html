The Best and Worse Case Scenarios For the Houston Rockets this Off-Season

The way I see it right now is the Rockets are at a crossroads regarding their future. It feels as if so much hinges on what happens this off-season. I know that’s still several months away, but this year feels like a lost cause at this point. Nothing is going to change until the final buzzer of the 82nd game sounds. That’s just the reality. So instead of talking about possible changes that they’ll never make, I want to shift my focus to when decisions will matter and actually be made. Houston can either reposition itself perfectly and return to relevancy in a few short months, or everything can go terribly wrong and then we’re back to square one. I wanted to explore both sides of a potential fate, so that there can be some expectations set. Basically, what are some realistic possibilities heading forward?

Best Case Scenario

I’ll start with the positive side. Of course, that begins with landing the #1 overall pick during the 2023 NBA Draft Lottery on May 16th. This would mean, most likely, Houston selects Victor Wembanyama to be one of its cornerstones for the foreseeable future. Wemby isn’t going to be traded, so there’s one starting spot filled. Next, we have to address all of the smoke around James Harden's potential return. I personally am not a fan of this (which you can read more about here), so I’m going to say that doesn’t happen. That leaves more money for other free agents to be considered. The people I like most out of the realistically available list are Jerami Grant, Caris LeVert, Gary Trent Jr., and D’angelo Russell. I think with $60,000,000+ in cap space we can sign at least two of them; I’ll pick Jerami Grant and D’angelo Russell for the purposes of this exercise. I also wouldn’t be mad if Fred VanVleet was in the mix as well.

Now we have a few other positions to fill via trades. There needs to be at least two trades made, in my opinion. The first being for a point guard. In a dream scenario, that would be LaMelo Ball. Ball is already in a tough situation with the Hornets, so it’s the perfect time for the Rockets to swoop in and make an offer they can’t refuse. That starts with Alperen Sengun and KPJ. Then I would throw in the Clippers’ pick from this season, and a combination of some of our picks and some of Brooklyn’s. Not giving them all away, though; maybe 3 or 4 total including the one from the Clippers. I feel honestly that would be enough. Lamelo isn’t at the star level yet to where the Hornets could demand much more. Then, we need another starting big. That could be a number of players, and we still should have some money to play around with considering 3 members of our starting lineup would still be on rookie contracts (Jalen Green, Lamelo, Wemby), so I’ll choose John Collins for a package of Kenyon Martin Jr., Tyty Washington Jr., and Tari Eason plus some amount of picks depending on how high Collins’ value really is.

Resulting Starters: LaMelo Ball, Jalen Green, Jerami Grant, John Collins, Victor Wembanyama

Resulting Bench: D’angelo Russell, Josh Christopher, Jae’Sean Tate, Jabari Smith Jr., Usman Garuba

Worst Case Scenario

Here’s where things can get rough. It’s fun to imagine what could happen to put us in the best possible situation, but there’s also a world where none of what was discussed above works out as intended. That starts with the Rockets falling all the way to pick #7 by passing the Spurs and Pistons in the standings before the end of the season. Will this happen? Probably not because it’s only a 7% chance that they do get that pick even if they finish 3rd worst, but it’s still a possibility. By that point in the draft you’re looking at Nick Smith Jr., Jarace Walker, Cam Whitmore, or Keyonte George, most likely. They’re still solid prospects, don’t get me wrong, but nowhere near a generational talent like Wembanyama. I’ll say they opt for Cam Whitmore and just keep him on the roster rather than trading the pick because, remember, this is the worst that could happen. Then, they strike out in free agency as well. Rumors are already building about no one wanting to come to Houston, and let’s just say that comes to reality and we end up overpaying a guy like Cam Johnson using most of our cap room. Maybe we’ll sign some smaller bench players as well.

The other thing to mention is that Stephen Silas might not be fired either. I know his time is long overdue, but if we’re going strictly off the worst that could happen. I would say that would be him staying around. It wouldn’t be that hard for Tilman Fertita to just go along with another “young-team” season with a terrible head coach as opposed to shelling out some extra cash for a better, more-respected one. I left this discussion out of the top part since so much has been covered on it already, but yes, the best case scenario would consist of an entirely new coaching staff outside of a few assistants like John Lucas and Mahmoud Abdelfattah. Also, the front office could just simply not make any trades either. They may view it as they don't have enough assets to put together a playoff-caliber roster and, as a result, just hold everything for the following off-season. I really hope that doesn’t happen, but I doubt we see major changes to the roster if Silas remains as the coach.

Resulting Starters: KPJ, Jalen Green, Cam Johnson, Jabari Smith Jr., Alperen Sengun

Resulting Bench: Daishen Nix, Josh Christopher, Tari Eason, Jae’Sean Tate, Usman Garuba, Kenyon Martin Jr.

Final Thoughts

I think what will actually end up happening is some kind of combination of the two scenarios outlined above. If Silas isn’t fired I believe there will be a riot in downtown Houston (sarcasm). It’s really getting to that point. There’s also no way the organization just sits there and does nothing after mentioning several times about “Phase 2” of the rebuild and with their draft pick being owed to OKC the following season. That being said, I’m also not a huge believer in the Lamelo Ball fantasy either. While it is possible, it’s a bit of a stretch. Whatever happens, the topic I’m most interested in keeping my eyes on is which members of our young core stick around, and which ones are moved to a new destination. There are just so many different pathways, and I can’t wait to watch it all unfold. At this point, it’s like the entire fanbase is just counting down the days until we can end this miserable season.
