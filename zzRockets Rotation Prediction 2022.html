Predicting The Rocket's Rotation For The 2022-2023 NBA Season

As the offseason buzz winds down, apart from the Kevin Durant situation, it seems like the proper time to predict what the Rocket’s rotation will look like next season. With 3 additional players added to the roster in the 2022 NBA draft it can be difficult to determine how many minutes each player should get. While the main goal of next year should be developing some of the younger players, many fans are eager for the team to take a step forward.

Resolving both the Christian Wood and John Wall situations will definitely have an impact in the team's lineup this upcoming season, but veterans like Eric Gordon and Boban Marjanovic remain on the roster. In fact, here’s is a full list of the roster as of the end of July and what I believe will be each player’s fate before the start of the season.

Guards

Jalen Green - kept

Kevin Porter Jr. - kept

TyTy Washington Jr. - sent to G-league

Eric Gordon - kept, possibly traded

Trey Burke - waived

Dennis Schroder (Unsigned Free Agent) - signs somewhere else

Garisson Matthews - kept

Daishen Nix - kept

Josh Christopher - kept

Fowards

David Nwaba - kept

Sterling Brown - waived

Jae’sean Tate - kept

Marquese Chriss - waived

Anthony Lamb - waived

Bruno Fernando - sent to G-league

Kenyon Martin Jr. - traded

Tari Eason - kept

Usman Garuba - kept

Jabari Smith Jr. - kept

Centers

Boban Marjanovic - kept

Alperen Sengun - kept

This leaves the Rockets with 13 players plus whatever they get in return for Kenyon Martin Jr. and possibly Eric Gordon. For the purpose of this article let's say that no player can get more than 36 minutes per game and that Stephen Silas chooses to run a 11-man rotation. The following is the number of minutes I expect each player to play next season per game. Keep in mind that I have no insider information, and this is all just speculation.

Starting Lineup

Kevin Porter Jr: 30 mpg

Jalen Green: 33 mpg

Eric Gordon: 23 mpg

Jabari Smith Jr: 26 mpg

Alperen Sengun: 30 mpg

Off The bench

Josh Christopher: 23 mpg

Jae’sean Tate: 20 mpg

Tari Eason: 17mpg

Garrison Matthews: 14 mpg

Daishen Nix: 13 mpg

Usman Garuba: 11 mpg

Boban Marjanovic: Spot minutes on some nights

David Nwaba: Minimal spot minutes on some nights

*mpg = minutes per game

If Eric Gordon were to be traded his minutes would likely be divided up among whoever they get in return plus Josh Christopher, Garrison Matthews, Daishen Nix, and Jae’sean Tate to be the replacement starter. I expect Marjanovic and Nwaba to receive very minimal minutes next season as the focus should be letting the young guys grow. TyTy Washington may replace Daishen Nix later in the season but is still unproven to this point.

I think that Josh Christopher will have an increased role as the 6th man next season and help lead the second unit along with Daishen Nix. Usman Garuba should finally get a chance to prove himself with Christian Wood gone playing behind Sengun. Tari Eason can hopefully build upon his success in Summer League and maybe work his way into a starting role come next season. The future is bright for this young squad and the proper rotation next season is a crucial step in turning potential into reality.
