Comparing the Houston Rockets' Current Rebuild to Similar Teams' Rebuilds In Recent Years

It’s no secret by now that the Houston Rockets have been in a rebuilding phase since the pivotal James Harden trade back on January 13th, 2021. Throughout this period, they have basically been grouped together with the Pistons, Magic, and Thunder as the tanking teams of the NBA. Because of this, they often get compared to each other. All 4 franchises have been at the bottom of the standings for at least 2 seasons at this point, and an evaluation is long overdue. How successful or unsuccessful have they been relative to their primary competition? That’s the question which I will attempt to answer with the following information.

Rockets

I’ll start here just to set a baseline. The Rockets currently have 2 top picks on the roster, Jalen Green at #2 in 2021 and Jabari Smith Jr. at #3 in 2022. They also have a plethora of other young talent from the recent drafts, such as Alperen Sengun, Tari Eason, and Usman Garuba. There is also the chance of adding another star-level prospect this summer, as Houston is currently tied for the worst record in the league. The Rockets haven’t had very much success on the court, though. In fact, they have owned the worst record out of anybody for the past 2 seasons. Whether this was by design or not, that statement still remains true. Another factor to mention is how much cap space will be available this off-season, around $60,000,000, which can be used to sign quality free agents to help guide this team in the future. The last thing to point out is that all of Brooklyn’s picks are owed to Houston (outright or via a swap) through 2027.

Pistons

Detroit landed Cade Cunningham with the #1 overall pick in 2021 and then Jaden Ivey subsequently with pick #5 a year later. Cunningham is on pace to be a future stud, and Ivey is having a solid rookie season in his own right. They also still have Bojan Bogdanovic on the roster, leading the team in scoring. Not to mention, their several other younger pieces like Saddiq Bey, Killian Hayes, James Wiseman, Jalen Duren, and Marvin Bagley III. The Pistons are the other team tied for the worst record as well, with the best shot at landing Victor Wembanyama that any organization can hope for. Their cap situation isn’t very good though. Whereas the Rockets can clear up to 75 million in space, the Pistons are stuck at about 26 million. That’s barely enough for one solid role player. From my understanding, Motor City does have control of their future first round picks, aside from owing the Thunder one with a bunch of protections on it through 2027, as well as possessing a fleet of second rounders that is too complicated to even understand.

Magic

Orlando also has a #1 pick, Paolo Banchero, along with several other key players from the draft like Jalen Suggs, Franz Wagner, and Cole Anthony. They have one of the youngest rosters in the league, which is pretty much the same for all 4 organizations in this comparison. I do also like some of their other young guys, such as Moritz Wagner, Wendell Carter Jr., Bol Bol, and even Markelle Fultz. Jonathan Issac also has some tremendous defensive potential, but has been out of the league for so long that it may take him a little bit to get back to full speed. However, they don’t have much cap space this summer to do anything with. The Magic are a considerable margin ahead of the Rockets in the standings, around 12 games, so that is a promising sign on their end. They have certainly been at least a respectable team, and the same could not be said about Houston at certain points this season. Jamahl Mosley, their coach, is also significantly more respected by fans than Silas is, which is another positive. I don’t see championship contention in their future unless drastic changes are made, but they have been successful so far in constructing a roster. We’ll just have to see how far that team can go over the next few years.

Thunder

OCK is often the team that upset Rockets fans point towards as an example of what we should be doing. They have an enormous amount of draft assets going forward, two top picks in Josh Giddey and Chet Homlgren, as well as a competent roster for the time being. The Thunder have really built up a good future in such a short amount of time. Jalen Williams has even risen to 2nd on the official Rookie of the Year ladder despite the fact that he wasn't picked until the 12th spot. I haven’t even mentioned Shai Gilgeous-Alexander yet. The 24-year-old guard is having an absolutely phenomenal season, averaging 31.2 points, 5.7 assists, 4.8 rebounds, 1.1 blocks, and 1.7 steals while making the all-star game for the first time in his career. It is also amazing that their record is 31-35 without Chet Holmegren in the lineup. The Thunder are easily in the best position moving forward, and there isn’t much else to it. Oklahoma City did have more players to trade away at the start of their rebuild, and have made the most of that advantage. They can legitimately make the playoffs this year and still have 19 firsts through 2029. That’s extremely difficult to do, and yet it has happened.

Final Thoughts

I think I would rank the teams based on who will win a championship, first to last, like this: Thunder, Rockets, Magic, Pistons. I’m probably biased, but I really would take Houston’s situation over the one in Orlando or Detroit. We just have so much more flexibility. I do have to applaud the Thunder, though. They have done such a great job orchestrating these last few seasons to perfection. I’m really interested to see which rebuilds work out and which ones don’t. Will the respective “star” players stay in their current locations or be moved for a proven veteran? There’s so much to watch. After 3 years, it’s time to start moving in the right direction towards contention. I know that’s a difficult process and it is one that will reveal more answers as time continues forward.
