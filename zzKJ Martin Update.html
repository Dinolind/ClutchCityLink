An Update On the Kenyon Martin Jr. Situation and What It Means For the Future

Kenyon Martin Jr. has certainly had his ups and downs with the Houston Rockets. Ever since he was drafted back in 2020 with the 52nd overall pick, he has proven himself time and time again. With a presumed trade request still looming, where does his future stand with the organization? Could Martin just be a 1 year rental, or is he someone that is a real part of Houston’s young core long term? For now, we don’t know the answer to any of those questions, but a lot of speculation has risen over the past few months. Just like everyone else on the roster, his role and situation is entirely unique, and should be explored in greater detail.

What We Know So Far

While Martin has been a large part of this team for the past 2 seasons, his role has been the opposite of clearly defined. One night, he’s a starter and the team’s leading scorer, and the next he plays like 10 minutes and barely makes much of an impact. He also doesn’t have a particular skill that jumps off the page. His athletic ability is impressive, but other than that, everything else is mediocre at best. That’s what makes him so difficult to classify. His position is also up in the air. There are stretches he plays at the 3, 4, and sometimes the small-ball 5. Maybe on a contender this versatility would be a good thing for his career, but on the Rockets, it just confuses everybody and makes him less appealing as a trade asset.

The former member of the Rio Grande Valley Vipers asked to be traded in the off-season and came into media day with the same energy. To my knowledge, this request still stands, though I do believe his relationship with everybody else in the organization has drastically improved since that day. The main reason he wanted to be traded in the first place was for the fear of not getting enough minutes, which is an issue we have yet to see with him this season. His performance over the first two weeks has also been decent. He even had one of his best games in the NBA against the Clippers on Halloween night. However, I will say the stats made him look better than he actually was, in my opinion.

Kenyon Martin Jr. Going Forward

I don’t even know exactly what to say here. As with a lot of other situations in the NBA, time will tell on this one. I do see the possibility of nothing happening being the most likely. If he was going to be traded this season, it would have happened by now. Maybe the organization is letting him put up quality numbers now before shipping him away at the trade deadline, but for some reason, I just don’t see that happening. The Rockets are trying to do right by their players, and using someone like that, goes against their principle that has been put in place. With each passing day, the chance of a trade occurring becomes less and less. The relationships are going to continue to mend, and the original reason for the request will continue to be addressed.

That being said, I don’t foresee Martin as a part of the roster when the Rockets hopefully win a championship. He’s just not going to fit anywhere. If there’s already concern about him getting minutes on the worst team in the league, then what’s going to happen when the franchise starts winning games. If anything, there will be even less time on the court to go around. Unless Martin majorly develops over the next few seasons, he is not at the tier of player that can start on a championship contender. Realistically, he would be the 3rd or 4th guy off the bench. Clearly Martin wouldn’t be content in that role, so something is eventually going to give. My best guess is that it will happen sometime between this off-season and the next one after that.

Conclusion

I think a perfect word to describe everything going on with these circumstances is unpredictable. It’s hard to tell what’s going to take place. To be honest, I was very shocked that Martin even asked to be moved in the first place. He doesn’t have the kind of influence to start making demands like that. I mean, what organization did he think would give him consistent starter type minutes. Maybe the Thunder or Pistons, but that’s about it. It’s odd that he would want to give up everything he has in Houston to play for yet another lottery team. Again, just like I said about Tari Eason, sometimes hitting on draft picks can be both a blessing and a curse. I guess we’ll see what happens, but my money is on him not being one of the lucky few who are a part of the Rockets roster that brings home an NBA championship.
