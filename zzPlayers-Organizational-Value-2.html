Which Rockets’ Players Currently Have the Most Value to the Organization (Ranked) Pt.2

Desperate times are upon us. At this point, it feels as if the Rockets won’t win a single game for the rest of the season. It’s honestly so disappointing that this is our current reality. I feel as if there is so much built up frustration that even the most loyal fans are beginning to doubt the future. There is just so little hope for anything positive to happen between now and the off-season, and it’s hard to even be surprised since we've been expecting it for a while now. Anyways, here’s the continued version of my rankings for the value different players possess according to the organization. I’ve already covered the first 4 guys here, so go check that out if you’re looking for the first half of the list or more of an introduction. Without further ado:

#5 - Alperen Sengun

The fact that I feel as if I have to place Sengun this low in itself is worrisome. Clearly, there’s a lack of faith in his abilities as a player. Most of our other young guys are given plenty of leeway to make as many mistakes as they want with little if no accountability. Sengun, however, is constantly benched and blamed for making the tiniest errors on defense when it’s not like the team has a chance to win regardless. A perfect example is a couple days ago against the Blazers where he was benched for the last 15+ minutes of the game despite having 17 points, 10 rebounds, and 5 assists up to that point. Now I’m not saying the first approach is the best one; it’s most likely not, but there needs to be some level of consistency for such a young team. I just have a fear that Sengun will be traded for pennies on the dollar the first chance we get because the front office doesn’t see hardly any value in his skillset.

#6 - Jae’Sean Tate

Jae’Sean Tate has become the de facto bench point guard since KPJ’s injury, for better or for worse. I have him higher than some other guys because he wasn’t traded at this most recent deadline, even though there were numerous rumors, showing that at least some establishment of value is still there. Tate has been decent, don’t get me wrong, but he’s also at a level where the organization doesn’t want to keep him around long-term. Again, like Sengun, he’s already got one foot out the door while the front office waits for someone else to offer a decent return for him. Whether that’s a better player or more middle tier draft capital, I’m not quite sure, hopefully the former. That’s just unfortunate for him because Tate has been a big contributor in some of our better lineups. His shot just needs to start falling for any opinions that matter to drastically change.

#7 - Kenyon Martin Jr.

Here’s another player that should, in theory, be a lot higher, but most times isn’t given the credit he deserves within the franchise. Kenyon Martin Jr. has had to work for everything this season. He’s been one of the best, most consistent players on a night-to-night basis, yet barely can secure a spot in the starting lineup if everyone is healthy. He’s already been on the trade block, presumably at least once, and it’s not unrealistic for it to happen again sometime soon. His organizational value just isn’t that high else we’d be seeing more offense generated through the duo of him and Alperen Sengun. KJ Martin is a lot more than just the high-flying dunks he has, and that needs to be put on display more. Sadly, I doubt that will happen unless we see some drastic changes, like hiring a new head coach.

#8 - Daishen Nix

I know what you’re thinking: why is Daishen Nix even on this list? To tell you the truth, I’m asking myself the same question. Why are we routinely seeing him get 30+ minutes in an NBA game? I’m sorry, but he’s just not who we should be prioritizing. I get he’s only still only 20 years old, but we have so many guys at that age to where it’s not really a factor for me anymore. I think the main problem is the people in charge refuse to let go of the belief that he’s some lottery-type talent. I mean, at some point we just have to move on. Basically everybody recognizes it except for the people who can actually make that decision. He’s incredibly overvalued. However, I do have to at least admit it. He hasn’t been benched permanently, traded, or sent back to the g-league, which has to show their beliefs, albeit completely against what most others agree upon.

Final Thoughts

Sorry if this felt more like a rant than anything, I guess I just needed some way to let it all out. For my honorable mentions, I have Josh Christopher, Usman Garuba, and Tyty Washington Jr. rounding out the list. I’m not quite sure on the order, but that feels about right. The rest of the players on the roster don’t exactly matter enough to the future for me to care. Trevor Hudgins and Darius Days spend most of their time with the RGV Vipers, Boban is just here for the good vibes, and Frank Kamisky was basically salary filler in a trade that, most likely, isn’t going to have very much of an effect long-term. I know all hope is not lost, but it’s really starting to feel that way. It’s like everybody is just counting down the days until the off-season. That is not how an NBA team should run, but here we are. This is what happens when a rebuild turns into an identity, and it isn’t pretty to watch.
