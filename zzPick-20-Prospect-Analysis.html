Incoming Prospect Analysis: What Should the Rockets Do With Pick Number 20

The 2023 NBA Draft is less than 2 weeks away. That means it’s time for the front office to start making decisions about who they do or don’t want from the incoming prospect class. Currently, the Rockets hold both the #4 and #20 picks. #4 is heavily favored to end up being Amen Thompson, but #20 is still up in the air. There is, of course, the possibility of a trade occurring using this asset, but it is still important to look at the available options if they were to end up using it themselves. However, knowing which guys will be available can be difficult to judge because there are 19 other picks that have to be made before the Rockets’ second selection. Regardless, here’s some of the players Houston should take a look at for this spot.

Jett Howard

Howard is a 6’8” shooting guard from the University of Michigan. He’s been linked to the Rockets through participating in multiple workouts with the team over these past few weeks. Everybody knows him for his shooting. In 29 games, he shot 49.6% from the field, 36.8% from 3, and 80% from the free throw line on decent-sized volume. He can spot up, run around in a motion-style offense while providing spacing, and hit tightly contested shots on a regular basis. That’s not all, though. His court vision and passing ability can’t be overlooked. However, he tends to take tough shots more often than he should, isn’t the best rebounder, and doesn’t have the same level of athleticism compared to some of the other players on Houston’s roster.

Bilal Coulibaly

Bilal Coulibaly is a 6’7” small forward from France who played alongside consensus #1 pick, Victor Wembanyama. The first thing you’ll notice is that he’s still incredibly young. In fact, he won’t even turn 19 until after the draft is over. That means he’s still incredibly raw as a prospect. Coulibaly is super athletic, a decent finisher at the rim, and has one of the highest defensive ceilings in his class. That being said, his jump shot is rather inconsistent, he struggles with turnovers, and needs to polish his overall game significantly. He’s not a player that would be directly inserted into the starting lineup. Maybe the small forward will even be sent to the g-league for a while to develop. If the Rockets want a project pick, this is their guy. His upside is tremendous, but it’s going to take a lot of time and effort for that to pan out.

James Nnaji

James Nnaji is a 6’10” center from Spain. Nnaji comes into this draft as a bit of an unknown. He plays overseas in a highly competitive Euro League, and hasn’t received very many minutes this past season as a result. From what we have seen, he seems to be a “rim-running” big man who’s good at setting screens and catching lobs. Think like a more explosive Bruno Fernando with a higher ceiling. If Ime Udoka still wants a contrasting big next to Alperen Sengun, then I think Nnaji fits that role perfectly. Given we have very little film to go based off of, most of his draft stock comes from his physical measurements and pure athleticism. His offensive game leaves a lot to be desired and a lot of development needs to occur before he can even become serviceable at the NBA level. This would be a risky pick for sure, but it’s also another chance to take a swing at landing a future star.

Kris Murray

Kris Murray is a 6’8” power forward from the University of Iowa who is the twin brother of current Sacramento Kings’ player, Keegan Murray. He is a graduating junior this year, meaning that he’ll be 23 years old by the time the season starts. This may be looked at as a negative to some, but it also means he will be on a rookie-level contract through his age 27 season. Murray has a polished defensive skill set that is based more around his basketball iq rather than pure athleticism. The same can be said on the offensive end. His biggest strength is being able to finish at the rim with seemingly minimal effort. His outside shot isn’t terrible either. Murray shot 34.8% from 3 during his 3 years at college. Basically, he’s a high-floor, low-ceiling guy that Houston should take if they want someone who can make an immediate impact towards winning games.

Final Thoughts

The Houston Rockets certainly have some tough decisions to make in the coming weeks. This is likely the last draft where the team will be in a position to make a single, if not multiple, first round selections. It could very well be the last chance we have to draft someone who will be a part of an eventual championship-caliber roster. As it stands currently, the 4th overall pick looks like it’ll be Amen Thompson and the 20th overall pick looks like it’ll be Jett Howard. If Howard is off the board by that point, I think a trade is a real possibility. The rotation simply doesn’t have room for another 19 or 20-year-old player that the organization isn’t completely sold on. Drafting another guard at 20 is almost completely out of the question. Other than that, we’ll just have to wait and see. These prospects are just a drop in the ocean of potential possibilities.
