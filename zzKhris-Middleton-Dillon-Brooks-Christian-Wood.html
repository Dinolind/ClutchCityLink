My Thoughts On Khris Middleton, Dillon Brooks, and Christian Wood As Signings For the Rockets

As the NBA playoffs continue, free agency draws closer. As you probably already know, the Rockets are projected to have upwards of $60 million in cap space this summer. That leaves the door wide open for basically any possibilities. The roster is going to change and improve dramatically as a result. Three names have recently been linked to Houston as potential options: Khris Middleton, Dillon Brooks, and Christian Wood. All 3 were a part of different disappointing teams this season, and are scheduled to hit the open market in July. Below are my thoughts on the matter at hand for each player as well as some predictions moving forward.

Khris Middleton

Before the Bucks flamed out in the first round of the playoffs, many assumed Middleton would most likely remain with the team. However, now Milwaukee is in an interesting situation. Middleton has a player option for the 2023-2024 season worth about $40,000,000. I doubt he declines this option without getting offered somewhere around that amount on a longer term deal. The Bucks can offer him up to 5 years/$272,000,000 on a max deal by having him decline the player option in favor of an entire new contract. For reference, that’s more money than they’re paying Giannis Antetokounmpo and the same amount the Nuggets are giving Nikola Jokic. The question is, does the organization want to give him that type of money? I believe the answer to that is no.

That’s where the Rockets come in. While we can’t offer the 5 year max, we can give Middleton 4 years at around $200,000,000. That’s simply an absurd figure that I simply don’t feel comfortable with. Middleton isn’t better than James Harden, and I’m heavily against even giving Harden that much. Plus, he hasn’t been able to stay healthy consistently in recent years and certainly isn’t getting any younger. He would fit in well with the starting lineup, but he’s just not worth a max contract. If the Bucks aren’t willing to offer him very much at all, then I could see a team like Houston getting him for around 35 million a season. I’m just not ready to pigeonhole the roster for a guy that can’t lead a team and is clearly declining in his ability.

Dillon Brooks

I have never seen a player be more universally hated, even by his own organization, more than Dillon Brooks. The Grizzlies literally came out and said that he wouldn’t be back with the team “under any circumstances.” I can’t even imagine what’s going on with that whole situation, nor do I want to find out. Memphis drafted him, has been his home in the NBA for several years, and now wants nothing to do with him. Why would the Rockets even want to bother with him in the first place? There seems to be very few pros and a ton of cons. We don’t need to bring yet another controversial figure into an organization trying to turn itself around. We’ve more than filled the quota with Kevin Porter Jr., Ime Udoka, and Tillman Fertitia.

Brooks also isn’t going to come very cheap. He’s reportedly seeking a $25 million dollar multi-year deal. While I believe any team would be out of their minds to offer him that kind of money, it especially doesn’t make sense for the Rockets. We need shooting, interior defense, and maturity with any free agent signing we make, and he just doesn’t offer any of those 3 qualities. Brooks shot just 39.6% from the field and 32.6% from 3 last season. The league averages were 47.5% and 36.1% respectively. Being a team that consistently struggles to hit outside shots, we certainly don’t need any more wings that can’t space the floor. It’s also not very smart to bring his type of locker room presence around such a young team.

Christian Wood

Christian Wood’s stint with the Dallas Mavericks didn’t go exactly as planned, to say the least. Although the counting stats don’t look too bad, he basically fell out of the rotation by the end of the year. That being said, I wouldn’t mind seeing him back in a Rockets jersey. Of course, this time he would be coming off the bench, but if the price is right, he wouldn’t be too bad of an option. That begs the question, though, of what is the right price? His average salary is currently around $14,000,000, and I expect him to stay close to that number. He hasn’t outperformed or underperformed since that contract was first signed, so I can’t imagine any reason why we see a major pay cut or increase.

However, something tells me he’d rather go to a contender that can offer him more of a chance at winning it all. After 4 consecutive seasons of missing the play-in or playoffs entirely, he’s going to get tired of losing eventually. There is also the off chance that he simply doesn’t want to come back to Houston. After being traded away from the team, I wouldn’t fault him for making that decision. He might not fit well with this roster either. We’re going to have so many big men already that deserve minutes, and we’re supposedly going after someone with more of a defensive presence in the paint. The bottom line is that while I’m not completely opposed to the idea of a Christian Wood reunification, I doubt it will happen.

Final Thoughts

Regardless of what the plans are now, they are likely going to shift dramatically once the NBA draft is all said and done. So much of the Rockets’ future depends on what happens over these next few months, and I can’t wait to see it all happen live. Middleton, Brooks, and Wood all have reasons for adding them to the roster, but I think there are still better options out there. We need players who can turn this team around and keep it there for several years to come. This is likely our one chance at adding multiple veteran pieces all at the same time, so we must seize the opportunity and make the most of it. Let’s just hope the people in charge make the right decisions.
