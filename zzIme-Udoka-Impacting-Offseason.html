How Ime Udoka Is Going to Impact the Rest of the Rockets' Off-Season

Ime Udoka is now the head coach of the Houston Rockets. It honestly surprised me how quickly the final decision was made, but I’m elated with the result. Udoka was the perfect candidate to mentor this young team. The future looks a lot brighter with him in charge instead of Stephen Silas. This hiring also marked the beginning of the Rockets offseason. We now have our head coach secured and the focus can shift towards something else. That something else can be broken down in 3 main categories: the draft, free agency, and trades. Below is how the hiring of Udoka may affect these.

NBA Draft

It’s no secret by now that this is the last year that Houston can actively try and build through the draft. I think the top 3 are solidified regardless of who was the head coach, but beyond that it’s likely going to vary now with Udoka. This is just a theory with no real evidence, but I suspect Houston may now decide to draft a wing over another guard. Udoka has experience coaching two younger wing players that have turned into all-stars, Jaylen Brown and Jayson Tatum. Since we’ve seen his formula with that type of player work out before, the front office may be more inclined to select either Cam Whitmore or Jarace Walker over Amen or Ausar Thompson.

As for our pick at #20, I believe now that there’s a higher possibility of that being traded than there was before. We now have a coach who’s going to want to add veterans and focus more on developing a core group of young guys, and drafting that late in the first round goes completely against those assumptions. It really doesn’t make any sense, especially considering we already have several later first round picks in Josh Christopher, Usman Garuba, Alperen Sengun, Tari Eason, and Tyty Washington Jr. All I’m saying is expect something to be done with the Clippers pick before the draft or on draft night. It’s an asset we shouldn’t let go to waste.

Free Agency

Udoka said at his introductory press conference that he wanted to add different types of big men around Alperen Sengun. More specifically, ones that can shoot. This has led to a bunch of fans pointing towards Brook Lopez as a potential option. Lopez is going to be an unrestricted free agent this offseason, and will likely command somewhere around the 12-15 million dollar mark per year on his new contract. Lopez is a great fit for this roster. He can shoot decently well, yes, but his main upside is on the defensive end. Other options may include Nikola Vucevic, Naz Ried, and Mason Plumlee.

Another point that was brought up was this team’s clear inability to make open shots. I’ve been saying this for months: we need more shooters. That seems to be a top priority heading into free agency. A few people that fit this role are Cam Johnson, Harrison Barnes, and Khris Middleton. There are also some lower tier guys that are available, such as Gary Trent Jr., Caris Levert, and Josh Hart. Additionally, there is hope that some of our own talent will progress in the shooting department as well. I’m looking at Jabari, Jalen, and Sengun as some of the people who need to work on their shot this off-season.

Trades

This one I’ll keep short because there are just so many possibilities that it’s impossible to discuss them all. I’ve seen Jalen Brown’s name being tossed around as the main trade candidate. It has been connected to the hiring of Udoka because the two have been connected for quite some time. Brown also speaks very highly of his former coach. Other options I’ve seen are RJ Barrett and Tyus Jones. I’m more in favor of the latter. Jones fits the bench guard role for this team perfectly. On the other hand, Barrett has been too inconsistent to garner any real consideration. Plus, he’s on a 4 year/$100,000,000 contract, and hasn’t lived up to that level so far.

What’s Next?

Getting Ime Udoka has checked off one of the biggest question marks we had heading into the summer. He was the best candidate in my eyes, and I’m glad the organization felt the same way. From here, the next thing to happen is the draft lottery. In my previous article I already went over some of the scenarios for what happens if the Rockets land at each position. After we know that, it becomes much easier to find potential trades that make sense for both sides. For example, if we know we’re getting Victor Wembanyama, then we could look to deal Sengun for a starting-caliber wing. Basically, we need to know what we have before we can figure out what we need moving forward. Regardless, this should be the most exciting offseason we’ve had in a while, and hiring Ime Udoka so soon may impact some of the outcomes.
