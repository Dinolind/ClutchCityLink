Is the National Criticism Warranted? Do the Rockets Still Have a Bright Future?

The Houston Rockets are not in a very good place right now, to say the least. They have only won 4 out of their last 31 games heading into the All-star break, and the signs of optimism have faded among basically every group of people. Fans, especially, have become extremely frustrated at the state of the organization currently, feeling like nothing can save these final 24 games from complete and total disaster. The concerning part, however, is that the criticism doesn’t stop there. A majority of national media figureheads and corporations have utterly bashed the Rockets over the past few months. Are these judgements accurate about there simply being no glimmer of hope for this franchise, or is it more of a case of overemphasizing something for attention? Below is what I consider an unbiased attempt at answering that question.

Criticism

Before I say anything, please note that these are not my comments and are just a general consensus among critics. The main point centered around everything is that the Rockets are on pace to have the worst record in the league for the third consecutive season, and are consistently getting blown out by teams that they should be at least somewhat competitive with. It’s obvious the players have a lack of effort, the coach has one foot out the door, and the GM refuses to make any moves to actually make the team perform better. Houston seems like a lost cause when it comes to successfully running a basketball team, and it shows. Even most of their young talent has made little progress and are only putting up increased numbers because, at the end of the day, somebody has to score.

Furthermore, just because everybody is young doesn’t mean they project to actually make any improvement. Right now it’s just a bunch of what ifs with no signs that anything is headed in the right direction. Even if the team strikes gold and lands Victor Wembenyama, it’s doubtful they’ll make any sort of push into the playoffs next season. This organization has simply been a disaster since James Harden left them. Their play has been awful on both ends of the floor and there’s just no reason to think that will change anytime soon. It’s becoming more and more likely, by the day, that this year’s team will finish with a worse record than last year, which should be a huge red flag. It’s going to take a lot more than just this offseason for most of the issues to be fixed. They could be looking at a 5-7 year rebuild at this point.

Facts

Right now it’s popular to use Stephen Silas as a scapegoat for a majority of the team’s struggles, and I tend to agree with that. His firing has been long overdue, but by now it’s unlikely anything will happen before the end of the season. There have been plenty of opportunities to instate a new head coach, yet for some reason nothing has been done. That reason is, most likely, because the front office and owner are so fixated on having the best odds at landing a top 2 pick in the lottery. Whether or not that’ll work remains to be seen, but keeping Silas certainly aids in pursuing that goal. If the strategy does work and the team does land Victor Wembenyama, all the losing might’ve been worth it. If it doesn’t, we still have a top 5 pick in a loaded draft class that can be used as a trade piece in a future deal.

Next up, of course, is the lack of development concern. This point I understand, but don’t completely agree with. The reality is no effort is being given, so it’s basically impossible to tell how much or how little a player has improved since last season. We can’t really assess Jalen Green’s, Josh Christopher’s, or Usman Garuba’s progression either due to a lack of opportunity or effort. In fact, both Garuba and Christopher have been rumored to not be giving 100% at every practice, but can you really blame given the current state of the team? The one guy who does consistently get minutes and put in the work, Alperen Sengun, has been progressing on a daily basis and even most media outlets acknowledge that fact. Even other guys who weren’t first round picks from last year either A struggle with determination, like Kevin Porter Jr., or B, have progressed, like Kenyon Martin Jr. and Jae’Sean Tate to an extent.

Verdict

Are the Rockets in a bad place currently? Yes. Are they doomed for the foreseeable future? No. There are still plenty of paths out of the present situation, but it may take a little bit longer than was originally expected. First off, we do have plenty of money to sign free-agents this offseason, or make trades for them if need be. Then there's the rumor about James Harden’s return, which I’m personally not a fan of, but it would almost instantly make this roster Play-in worthy, if not better. We also have some valuable draft assets from the Brooklyn Nets to include in any deal as well. Not to mention, the huge pool of young players that basically cannot all remain on the same team forever. If we wanted to go all-in, there’s plenty of assets to make us at least a respectable playoff team, but the goal is to win championships. That’s the reason why I’m not overly worried, because I know good things take time and persistence.
