Is Alperen Sengun Fit to Be the #1 Option On the Houston Rockets?

There has been a growing sentiment over these past few weeks that Alperen Sengun should take over as the primary offensive option for the Houston Rockets. It has become apparent that changes need to be made regarding their playstyle because the team is currently on an 8-game losing streak, making them dead last in the entire league. This is obviously very frustrating to watch and has led to an immense amount of disappointment among fans, to the best of my knowledge.

Right now, I would assume that Jalen Green is considered the #1 guy for this team, followed closely by Kevin Porter Jr. at #2. Sengun is probably in the range of 3-5 depending on how he is ranked among the rest of the starters. Anyways, this would be a big jump and increase his role significantly during only his second year in the NBA. Porter may be currently injured due to a collision that happened on January 11th, but there is no further update at the time of writing. As a result of the point guard potentially being out, more plays would have to be generated elsewhere. That likely wouldn’t be Green because he’s known as a score-first guard, not a facilitator. The next best option beyond him is Sengun.

Case For

It’s time to hand over the keys to Sengun. The offense flows so much better when he has the ball in his hands, and it’s not like we have a real point guard to get everyone involved anyways. Additionally, his shooting liabilities are concealed further when he’s the one making passes rather than the one on the receiving end. We do not need to waste his unique skill set by turning him into exclusively a pick-and-roll big man who doesn’t do much else. That would be detrimental to his development. He has a huge amount of talent that needs to be utilized properly. There’s no sense in making him Clint Capela 2.0 when we don’t have the specific type of player that Capela needs to be successful.

Furthermore, the NBA big-man landscape as a whole is shifting into a new direction. There are fewer traditional centers by the day, and the ones that do remain often fail under many different situations (cough cough, Rudy Gobert, cough cough). There have also been other stars across the association, such as Nikola Jokic, reigning back-to-back MVP, and Anthony Edwards suggesting Sengun should be more of a focal point in Houston. Jokic himself said, “I think they need to play a little bit more through him (Sengun). This guy has the talent. He can pass the ball, he can post up, and he has the touch around the rim” and Edwards recently stated, “Me personally, I think they need to build around No. 28 (Sengun). Jalen (Green) and KPJ (Kevin Porter Jr.) are franchise players also, but that dude is something serious. We had to game plan for him.”

Quotes used can be found here.

Case Against

Sengun shouldn’t be our top guy this soon into his career. Both Jalen Green and Jabari Smith Jr. were drafted higher than him, and in theory have more potential. They needed to be given the most opportunity because more was invested in them. Besides, Sengun was coming off the bench just last season and hasn’t shown enough improvement to warrant that kind of promotion. His defense is still below average and he is always in foul trouble. We can’t play through somebody who can’t stay on the court. He has his strengths, but they are often overshadowed by his weaknesses. There has to be a very specific roster in place to build around someone who doesn’t shoot very well, and it’s not the time to make those kinds of moves.

Additionally, Sengun is just too inconsistent compared to someone like Eric Gordon or KPJ. Some nights he looks to be running the show and other times he finishes with a total of 5 points. I know that would change if he was given more touches, but it is still a factor to consider. What’s more is that Sengun may not even be a center in the first place. He’s more like a power forward and needs more of a true big-man alongside him. That shouldn’t be Bruno Fernando, and Smith probably works better at the 3. This means an acquisition would have to be made, and it’s not worth giving up assets until we are certain he can handle that kind of responsibility.

Conclusion

I personally still think that Jalen Green should be the face of the franchise, and also believe it will stay that way for a while. Sengun should be more involved, though. I hate to see him standing around with seemingly nothing to do, unless he’s out there with the bench unit. We need to make serious changes, but I’m afraid it won’t happen. Right now there’s very little hope. Coach Silas finally made a positive change by not playing 3 centers when Jae’Sean Tate returned from injury, but then immediately went back to that lineup just a few games later. A player hierarchy needs to be established and that also means some players will end up at the bottom. Sadly, it seems like everything is likely to stay the same barring some unforeseen circumstances, like a major injury or head coach replacement.
