Which Rockets’ Players Currently Have the Most Value to the Organization (Ranked) Pt.1

The focus on the Houston Rockets has already begun to shift towards the NBA off-season despite the fact that we still have 23 games left. Unfortunately, I have fallen guilty to this trap as well. Big changes are coming soon, knock on wood, and it’s hard not to think about the future. However, we must first analyze what we have now in terms of value before moving forward. This will paint a clearer picture of which guys are here to stay, and who could be headed elsewhere as soon as this summer. At the end of the day, the NBA is a business and different guys have different amounts of value to this organization. Below is my attempt at ranking these perceived values based on information that has been made publicly available.

#1 - Jalen Green

Ok, I had to get the obvious out of the way first. Jalen Green is the player who is held amongst the highest regard within the organization. He was drafted in 2021 with the 2nd overall pick and has basically been viewed as the franchise player ever since. That being said, I don’t think that fact will ring true for much longer. If full belief was placed in Green, then we wouldn’t still have the worst record in the association. It just doesn’t make sense for the organization to continue losing if they think they already have their #1 option on a future championship-caliber team. Now that doesn’t mean he’s going to be traded. Just that his position as “the guy” in Houston is most likely in jeopardy.

#2 - Kevin Porter Jr.

Here’s where some people may start to disagree, but remember, this is not my opinion of what it should be, more of what I believe the front office’s perception actually is. Kevin Porter Jr. has been another focal point of this offense since he was called up from the RGV Vipers, for better or for worse. The simple observation that the experiment of placing him at point guard alongside Jalen Green is still continuing to this day, says a lot to me personally. Mainly, that they still believe very highly in him and his development in that role, even though all the signs point to it just simply not being the right fit. That, and of course him soon being considerably the highest-paid player on the team, currently, with an extension that starts next season.

#3 - Jabari Smith Jr.

Jabari Smith Jr. hasn’t had the best rookie campaign so far, but hopefully he can turn it around in this final stretch of games. It might be tough, though, considering the head coach is literally on record stating that he doesn’t run plays for him. Since we also now know Rafael Stone is involved a lot in the decisions made on the court, it almost made me want to put Smith lower. It’s just not very encouraging to hear as a fan that the entire season we spent losing last year amounted to a guy that doesn’t have much support from within the organization. Smith has seemingly lost his ability to shoot, and probably wouldn’t have much value at all by this point if he wasn’t a top-3 pick in this most recent draft. Anyways, I doubt Smith will be moved anytime soon because no other team is going to give up much for someone who hasn't performed well at the NBA level. We need to see some flashes first before anything can really happen.

#4 - Tari Eason

On the contrary, Tari Eason has been one of the very few bright spots this season. He has that certain hustle factor, which is extremely difficult to teach, and knows when and where to be on the court. It’s honestly starting to upset me that he isn’t starting by now. I can tell that he’s loved by members within the organization just based on interviews, and his value around the league has also grown tremendously. Is the only reason he’s not being given more opportunity because of the position he was drafted at? That seems a little short-sighted. Eason has also built up a good reputation with fans. I’m starting to think they’re limiting his minutes to cover up the flaws in his game and boost his trade value. Don’t be surprised if that’s what’s really going on because no other logical explanation can be given.

The value of each player according to the people running the Rockets is an incredibly important topic that should be explored further. After all, they’re the ones making the decisions. These opinions are going to change as time continues on, but this full complete list should give everyone a good idea heading into this summer. There are also plenty of possible scenarios to look at, which I’m sure I’ll cover eventually. We’re in such a unique spot where everything could either turn around perfectly or crash and burn into a pile of misery. It just comes down to how much we believe in the front office to make the right moves for the long-term future.
