2023 NBA Draft Preview: From the Houston Rockets’ Perspective

Draft week is upon us. On Thursday Night we will finally reap the reward of an objectively awful season through and through. With a new coaching staff, plenty of cap space, and a plethora of young players, the Rockets are poised to make a drastic turnaround as soon as next season. Before we get there, though, it is important to focus on the task at hand: making the best decisions with our draft picks. Houston currently holds picks 4 and 20, but nothing is set in stone. The front office has made at least one move on draft night every year since Rafael Stone became general manager, and I expect this year to be no different. There are so many moving parts in play that almost anything is a possibility.

Predictions

Most mock drafts have the Rockets selecting Amen Thompson 4th overall behind some order of Victor Wembanyama, Scoot Henderson, and Brandon Miller. I think this is, and will remain, the case. It probably goes without saying, but if any of those top 3 guys fall to number 4 instead, I expect them to be picked up by Houston. Even though Amen Thompson may be a questionable prospect in some eyes, including my own, he appears to be the best chance we have at landing a future superstar. His shooting is questionable at best, and it’s almost certainly going to take several years for him to reach his full potential. While there are a few other options, like Ausar Thompson, Cam Whitmore, or Jarace Walker, I just can’t foresee the people in charge passing on Amen.

As far as pick 20 goes, a trade is almost bound to happen. The roster simply doesn’t have room to bring in another young player, considering we already have a total of 8 first round picks in the last 3 years when you factor in our number 4 selection. That’s already over half the entire roster and 8/10 slots filled in a standard 10-man rotation. If you are interested in some prospects we could draft should we keep the pick, check out this article. Anyways, that begs the question about what we should trade number 20 for. The answer is probably future draft capital. I think if someone offers a 2025 protected first round pick, that deal gets done almost instantly for the simple reason of us prioritizing liquid assets over another young rookie. The other option is to try and package number 4 with number 20 to trade up for number 2 and Scoot Henderson, but as the days go by it seems like that possibility is becoming less and less likely.

What Else to Watch For

I mentioned earlier how the top 3 guys in this draft have almost been established before we know for sure. However, that doesn’t mean the top 3 teams taking those prospects will remain the same. The Spurs aren’t letting go of #1 basically no matter what, but both the Hornets and Trailblazers have interest in potentially trading their pick. Charlotte already has LaMelo Ball, meaning they are less likely to take the consensus number 2 guy, Scoot Henderson. Portland, on the other hand, is looking to transition into “win-now” mode, and really isn’t interested in drafting another young prospect whatsoever. This means we’re likely to see some movement in the top 3, and Houston may benefit by having Scoot Henderson fall to number 4.

There’s also the possibility of us trading out as well. If the front office wants to start winning as many games as possible next season, we could see the Rockets making a big move for a star, like Jaylen Brown. Especially if Brandon Miller is the one left at 4. We already drafted Jabari Smith Jr. last year, and both players fit mostly into the same archetype. Even if Houston isn’t the team directly involved in securing a big name, they can still serve as a third team facilitator with plenty of cap space, and may acquire an extra pick in the process for their services. If we do somehow keep the 2 picks we have now and draft 2 more rookies, it’ll be interesting to see which positions those players play and how they’ll impact the roster going forward. For example, if we draft Amen Thompson, Kevin Porter Jr. is either going to be traded or moved to a 6th man role.

Final Thoughts

The NBA Draft will certainly be interesting to watch. The good news for us is that the 2nd round can be skipped if you’re only interested in the Rockets. We aren’t buying a 2nd rounder or trading for one due to the roster constraints mentioned earlier. After the pick happens at number 4 and a trade is made with number 20, the night will pretty much be over. For my official predictions, I’ll say the Rockets take Amen Thompson at number 4 and trade 20 for a quality, rotational center like Jaxson Hayes or Daniel Gafford. If they do draft at number 20, I think they’re most likely to select Jett Howard. Nothing else seems as likely at the current moment, but we won’t know for sure until the time comes. One thing I can promise is that we will walk away with something.
