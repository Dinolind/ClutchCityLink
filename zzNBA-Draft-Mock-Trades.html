3 Mock Trades That Would Position Houston Differently In the NBA Draft

The NBA Draft is now less than a month away. On June 22nd, a new group of talent will officially enter the league. Chances are, the Houston Rockets will be in a position to land one of the top guys available. However, their current spot at #4 isn’t set in stone by any means. They have the potential to move up, down, or out entirely of the order. Today, I’ll propose some options for the first two case scenarios. Keep in mind that whatever follows is my personal opinion, though rooted in reality, so feel free to disagree as you will. Who knows? Houston may just stay at the 4th pick after all, but every possibility should always be explored, especially during such a pivotal offseason.

Trade #1

Magic receive: Kevin Porter Jr., 2023 first round pick from Houston (pick 4), 2023 first round pick from Houston via Los Angeles Clippers (pick 20)

Rockets receive: Jonathan Issac, 2023 first round pick from Orlando (pick 6), 2023 first round pick from Orlando via Chicago (pick 11)

This first trade represents a possibility for Houston trading down. From Orlando’s perspective, they move up in the draft while retaining 2 first round picks total. The real question here is how much they value a prospect available at 4 versus one available at 6. I think if Brandon Miller drops, this deal becomes almost a no-brainer. They desperately need a small forward to play alongside Paolo Banchero, and Miller fits that role perfectly. There’s also still plenty of options available at #20. Plus, they finally get off of Jonathan Issac’s contract, a player who is reportedly going to be on the trade block this summer.

Houston is able to flip 1 lottery pick into 2 without giving up any major assets. If they aren’t very high on whoever’s left between Amen Thompson, Brandon Miller, and Scoot Henderson, then a trade like this makes sense. I think Cam Whitmore and Jarace Walker would both fit well on this roster moving forward, so recouping some assets while trading down doesn’t sound like such a bad idea. We are giving up Kevin Porter Jr., but if a James Harden reunion is bound to happen anyway, that won’t really matter. Plus, Issac provides some much-needed defense and is still just 25 years old.

Trade #2

Hornets receive: Alperen Sengun, 2023 first round pick from Houston (pick 4), 2023 first round pick from Houston via Los Angeles Clippers (pick 20)

Rockets receive: Gordon Hayward, 2023 first round pick from Charlotte (pick 2)

The Hornets hold the 2nd overall pick, and rumors have already been circulating about how much longer that statement will remain true. They already have LaMelo Ball on their roster, so drafting Scoot Henderson, another ball-dominant guard, doesn’t make a whole lot of sense. They could draft Brandon Miller, but why not attempt to extract some additional value for doing so? Not only are they getting off of Gordon Hayward’s huge contract, but they also gain multiple first round picks and a center with all-star potential. That’s about as much as Charlotte can ask for.

For the Rockets, it all depends on how much more valuable Scoot Henderson is compared to Amen Thompson. If the organization truly believes that he’s a generational talent, then we should be willing to give up a lot for the rights to draft him. I know Sengun has the potential to be special, but we must realize Charlotte is going to want something of value in return for us to move up 2 spots. Although the Clippers’ pick is nice, it’s not enough in itself, considering how valuable Henderson currently is. Hayward’s contract is a lot to stomach, though, acquiring someone like him on a massive expiring deal is a great way to roll-over our cap space until next summer. He will also fill the starting small forward role for the time being.

Trade #3

Trailblazers receive: Mikal Bridges

Nets receive: Anfernee Simons, Kevin Porter Jr., Jae’Sean Tate, 2023 first round pick from Houston (pick 4), 2024 first round pick from Houston via Brooklyn (unprotected)

Rockets receive: Ben Simmons, 2023 first round pick from Portland (pick 3)

The Trailblazers get Mikal Bridges to pair with Damian Lillard for Anfernee Simons and a first round pick. Honestly, Portland should be looking to enter a rebuilding phase, but if they’re determined to remain competitive this might be the best trade available. Bridges has the potential to be an All-star next season, and Lillard has never played with anyone of that caliber. Yes, the Blazers are giving up their second best player in Simons. However, they make this trade 10/10 times if Bridges turns out to be the real deal. One possible workaround, if necessary, is to do a sign-and-trade with Jerami Grant instead.

Although the Nets have been reluctant to trade Bridges, their front office is going to have to choose a direction eventually. This trade gives them 2 young, future stars in Simons and whoever they select with the 4th pick plus control over their own pick next year. That last part, in particular, allows them to bottom-out and land another top prospect in 2024. Kevin Porter Jr. and Cam Thomas could also form one of the most lethal scoring backcourts in the league. Granted, anything else would be a bonus at best. Lastly, the Ben Simmons anchor gets moved onto another team, opening up flexibility for years to come. I’m sure the front office would be more than happy to sacrifice some potential value for Bridges in exchange for getting rid of Ben Simmons. The current situation is that bad.

For the Rockets, the incentive is easy: the draft rights to Scoot Henderson. Odds are, Charlotte is going to talk themselves into Brandon Miller one way or another. 1 Nets’ pick for next season and a couple of non-essential players to move up in the draft probably sounds a lot better than letting go of Alperen Sengun. Jae’Sean Tate has become the odd-man-out at this point, and KPJ is headed in that direction. His days as the starting point guard are almost certainly behind him, so we’ll have to see what happens there. The Simmons contract is arguably the worst in the league, but we have to use our cap space somehow. If that’s what it takes to get Henderson, then so be it. Regardless, after next season, he’s on the last year of his deal and becomes a tradable piece once again.

Conclusion

I’m personally the biggest fan of trade #3 if Henderson is still going to be on the board. If the Nets tank next season, that only makes the other picks we own from them in 2025, 2026, and 2027 more valuable. I also wouldn’t mind giving Sengun to the Hornets if we could move up to #2. That trade would result in a starting 5 of Scoot Henderson, Jalen Green, Gordon Hayward, Jabari Smith Jr., and a free agent center (potentially Brook Lopez) heading into next season. Then we would sign a nice 6th man and trade some other young players to improve the bench. For now, though, we just have to play the waiting game. I doubt anything will be done until we get much closer to draft day. One thing for sure though: it’s going to be exciting to watch.
