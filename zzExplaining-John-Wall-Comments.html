Explaining the Impact of John Wall’s Recent Comments About the Houston Rockets

As you’ve probably heard by now, John Wall recently sat down with Theo Pinson and AJ Richardson on the Run Your Race podcast to discuss his entire journey. Some of his comments were directed towards the Rockets and his time spent in Houston, and that’s what I’ll be focusing on today. Wall was bought out by the organization last offseason and has recently signed with the Clippers in hopes of winning a championship. That decision was made after Wall sat out the entirety of the previous season despite the fact that he wasn’t injured. He even got paid over $40 million dollars to do so.

The situation with him in the first place is one of the weirdest things I’ve seen in the NBA for a while. Russell Westbrook was swapped for Wall and a first round pick, when the Rockets first tried to retool around James Harden. This didn’t work and Harden was traded within the first month of the season. The backcourt with him and Victor Oladipo also failed, though mostly this time it was due to injuries, and then Wall was shut down a bit later for the rest of the season, which would end up being the last time he ever suited up for Houston.

You can listen the original podcast here.

Wall’s Comments

The Rockets are not in a good place right now, and Wall going onto a podcast and talking bad about the franchise just kind of felt like him rubbing salt in the wounds. Apparently he told teammates, “This is not how the NBA is. It’s a bad organization right now. They’ve got to fix some s***.” Followed by, "The s*** y’all getting away with over there, if you go to any other teams you’d be out of the f***** league." Clearly he was not a fan of what was going on, but that doesn’t mean he had any issue with cashing his paycheck.

Wall also accused the Rockets of “trying to lose on purpose, tanking,”even throwing shade at former players, stating, “We was starting n***** named Justin Patton.” This also came off the heels of Wall being asked to come off the bench, to which his response was, “For who? No offense, but for who? I was your best player last year.” The idea was for Wall backup Kevin Porter Jr. so he and Jalen Green could learn to coexist in the backcourt. Apparently Stephen Silas asked Wall about this by saying, “this is what the GM wants. He (Rafael Stone) wants you to play 10-15 minutes a game, sometimes don’t play,” to which Wall replied, “I’m not doing that. Either you’re going to guarantee me some minutes, or I’m not. Let (a player) earn his spot.” Then, supposedly, Silas said that Wall didn’t deserve that kind of treatment.

What’s Next

Stephen Silas actually ended up responding to these remarks, which he shouldn’t have done in the first place, by stating, “obviously, the organization decided that the rebuild was going to be underway…and (we) came to the conclusion that him not playing would be the solution.” This suggests that the organization, in particular, Rafael Stone, may have a lot more power over how things are run than most fans know about. Adam Spolane, Rocket’s insider, even confirms this statement. Maybe Silas is not the one to blame, after all, and it’s really the people above him making the decisions. I guess we may only know if/when Silas is ever fired and the new coach basically implements the same strategy.

In the grand scheme of things, Wall’s accusations aren't really going to change much. The team is still going to be terrible on offense and worse on defense. The effort is lacking by basically everyone on the court, and it seems like once the other team gets the lead, Houston just gives up. At this point, the only thing worth looking forward to is the draft lottery. Silas likely won’t get fired before the end of the season, no matter how much criticism he continues to receive, and it’s not like an interim would help very much if management is controlling it all anyways. These are tough times for the franchise, and that’s why I think so many people are jumping on the bandwagon to bash the Rockets. It’s sad, but I don’t really see anything making a turn for the better before this season ends. Prepare yourselves, these next few months are going to be brutal.

Final Thoughts

While I do think part of John Wall’s slanderous remarks are justified, he’s also on the tail end of his career and may just be looking to stir everything up one last time. Why didn’t any of this come out when it was actually happening? There could be some ulterior motives that may reveal themselves in the coming weeks. I don’t think the Rockets are in as bad of a situation as most media outlets will portray. They have plenty of young talent, future draft capital, and a ton of cap space this summer. The main problem now is that there’s no urgency to win. Once the pressure is on next season, I predict we will see some changes and some progress will actually be made. There’s just no incentive for players to be competitive outside of their own pride, and the higher ups are basically encouraging them to lose anyways. I just hope we aren’t in similar circumstances come this time next year.
