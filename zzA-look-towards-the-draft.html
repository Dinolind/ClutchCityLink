A Look Towards the 2023 NBA Draft From the Houston Rockets’ Perspective

The Houston Rockets have found themselves towards the bottom of the league once again, and one of the only positives resulting from that statement is a high pick in what looks to be an absolutely loaded draft class. Now that the season is over halfway done, it’s time to start exploring the different possibilities the Rockets’ might face when June 22nd (date of draft) rolls around. Unlike other major sports leagues, the top 14 picks in the NBA are handed out by chance rather than a fixed order based on the organizations’ final records. This can be nerve-racking as a fan, but it does keep things interesting and disincentivizes a team from losing every game on purpose if there is an incoming generational talent. Anyways, below are the different outcomes that are likely to occur based on everything we know as of now.

Land Victor Wembenyama or Scoot Henderson

The guy everybody really wants this year is the 7’ 2”, yes, 7' 2”, basketball phenom from France, Victor Wembenyama. Most media outlets across the nation have labeled him as the most hyped prospect since Lebron James. I believe it’s warranted too. At the age of only 19, there’s very little flaws in his game, and he’s basically guaranteed to have a hall-of-fame career if he can stay healthy. However, the chances of landing him are pretty slim, 14% to be exact, even if Houston is the worst team in the association. This is because under the new draft lottery rules, established in 2019, the bottom 3 teams all get the same chance at the top selection. Since then, the worst team hasn’t gotten to select #1 overall even once.

Another outstanding player, Scoot Henderson is also up for grabs. He’s not quite as ground-breaking as Wembenyama is, but analysts say he’d be the first guy to have his name called in pretty much any other year. Henderson is special and I think he would work perfectly with the Rockets. We already need a new guard, if the organization ever decides to give up on the KPJ point guard experiment, and the perfect person to slide into that role is the current g-league Ignite star. Henderson is not the best shooter, but him and Jalen Green in the backcourt would be one of the most explosive and exciting duos in the league. That pair would strike fear into the hearts of opposing teams. However, the Rockets would have just a 13.4% chance at Henderson as opposed to a 14% chance at Wembenyama. There’s still a possibility, though, and Jalen Green was selected by Houston second, after they had the worst winning percentage out of everyone in 2021.

Other Possibilities

If we go ahead and assume that the Houston Rockets will finish with the worst record again this season, which seems pretty likely at the point, the lowest pick they can get is #5. Unfortunately, it’s actually more likely for them to pick 5th, 47.9%, than it is for them to land in the top 3, 40.1%. Something tells me this is the year for Houston’s luck to finally run out, and we end up falling to number 5; just because the last two years we’ve avoided that reality. Statistically, the odds say that we will be choosing 5th. If that were the case, who are the next guys to start analyzing after Wembenyama and Henderson. Well, it’s definitely not as clear cut, but there are some names like Ausar and Amen Thompson, Brandon Miller, and Nick Smith Jr., who stand out.

To be honest, I don’t know much individually about these guys yet, so feel free to do more research on your own if you want to. I do know that 3 of them are guards: Thompson twins, and Smith Jr. I think it’s most likely for the Rockets to draft another backcourt player to pair with Green if we don’t get the number one spot. This could mean KPJ’s days as the team’s starting 1 are numbered. Any of these players would be great to add to the roster, and, who knows, some other prospects may emerge as we get closer to draft day. Although we also have the Bucks’ first rounder this year, it’s just way too soon to start picking favorites outside of the top 10. The later picks shuffle around a lot every year, so I wouldn’t even waste your energy.

Conclusion

We’re not getting Victor Wembenyama. Start believing that now so that it’s not a disappointment when it doesn’t happen. If it does, then it can be a surprise, not an expectation. I think Houston should be fine with whoever they do end up drafting, and we should not be worried about what happens if we don’t land one of the top 2 talents. There are a lot more internal issues we should be focusing on, rather than looking for a savior out of a current 18 or 19 year-old kid. Star players can help, yes, but no one has ever won it all in the NBA by themselves. Run as many tankathon spins as you want; just know that we won’t know for sure until it’s official. It’s more important to fix what we already have before we get too attached to possibilities. Right now that’s all the draft is, possibilities.
