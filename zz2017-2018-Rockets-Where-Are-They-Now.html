2017-2018 Houston Rockets’ Players: Where Are They Now 5 Years Later?

The 2017-2018 NBA season for the Houston Rockets has gone down as one of the best in franchise history. That team took the infamous Golden State Warriors to a 7-game series in the Western Conference Finals. Although that roster ultimately came up short, it was still one of the most exciting moments as a fan watching those last few games. Since then, every single player from that team is no longer with the Rockets, which begs the question: “Where are they now?” There were 5 main contributors that I’ll be discussing today: James Harden, Chris Paul, Eric Gordon, PJ Tucker, Clint Capela.

James Harden: Philadelphia 76ers

James Harden, undoubtedly the star player, now plays in Philadelphia. In large part because of him, the 76ers look poised to go on a deep playoff run this season. He has formed a big 3 along with Joel Embiid and Tyrese Maxey. Harden is currently averaging 21.4 points, 6.3 rebounds, 10.8 assists, 0.5 blocks, and 1.2 steals per game in about 37 minutes. Those are all-star level numbers, but alas, he didn’t make the list. While the former Rockets isn’t at the level he used to be, there’s no doubt that he’s still one of the league’s premier players. There are even rumblings of a potential reunification with his former city this off-season.

Chris Paul: Phoenix Suns

Chris Paul is what set that team apart from the ones that came before and after. He was able to control Harden’s ego and provide stability, a veteran presence, and much needed facilitation for the others. Many said the backcourt of these two wouldn’t work at all, and yet they were able to put their differences aside for that one year. Unfortunately, Paul was traded to the Thunder after the 2019 season and to the Suns after that. He now plays with Kevin Durant, Devin Booker, and Deandre Ayton, putting up numbers of 13.7 points, 4.3 rebounds, 9.1 assists, 0.4 blocks, and 1.6 steals a night. The Suns have a serious chance of winning it all, so we could potentially see a Harden-Paul matchup in the finals.

Eric Gordon: Los Angeles Clippers

Eric Gordon was the most recent member to be moved, which came at the most recent trade deadline. Gordon now plays with Paul George and Kawhi Leonard as part of the Clippers. There has been a 15-game sample size so far in which he’s averaging 10.5 points, 1.7 rebounds, 2.3 assists, 0.5 blocks, 0.6 steals per game on 45% from the field and 41.4% from 3. Those are impressive shooting splits, especially when considering he’s been given less opportunity than with Houston. The Clippers would be the 4th seed in the West if the playoffs started today. They certainly have the roster to go far, it just comes down to whether they can put everything together when it matters most.

Clint Capela: Atlanta Hawks

Capela was one of those guys who was a big part of that roster, but never got the credit he truly deserved. There was a period of time when diehards considered him the 3rd best player, behind Harden and Paul, yet most casual NBA fans wouldn’t have even recognized him. Anyway, he now plays for the Atlanta Hawks with Trae Young, Dejounte Murray, and John Collins. The Hawks may be looking to move Capela soon to open up minutes for Onyeka Okongwu, their backup center. Atlanta is also trying to compete this year because they changed their head coach and traded a good portion of their future for Murray last summer. Who knows? Maybe we’ll see a Hawks-76ers series in the first or second round.

PJ Tucker: Philadelphia 76ers

You simply can’t talk about the 2018 Rockets without mentioning PJ Tucker. He was the player that brought this team together. The level of hustle and intensity Tucker brought was unmatched among almost anyone in the league. He now plays for the 76ers with James Harden. Harden actually took a pay cut on his most recent contract just so that Tucker could be signed as well. He’s not having the best statistical season, putting up only 3.4 points, 4.0 rebounds, 0.8 assists, 0.2 blocks, and 0.6 steals per game, but he is 37 years old and most of his impact doesn’t show up on the stat sheet regardless. If this 76ers team can win the championship, both Tucker and Harden will finally get the first, coveted ring.

Final Thoughts

This year’s playoffs are set to be an absolute classic and it’s interesting to watch former Houston players compete elsewhere. I’m just happy that all 5 of these guys realistically have a chance to win the Larry O’Brien Trophy instead of wasting their careers with a rebuilding franchise. The current Rockets aren’t doing very well, so it’s nice sometimes to look back at better times. People will always remember that season as “what could’ve been”, but I challenge you to realize what it was: a great collection of players who were one injury away from taking down the greatest team in NBA history.
