Why Tari Eason Should Be the 5th Starter On the Rockets Over Jabari Smith Jr.

Tari Eason was drafted by the Houston Rockets last June with pick #17 out of LSU. He’s actually the first player in return from the massive draft capital haul via the Brooklyn Nets and has been seriously impressive this season during his rookie campaign. Despite that, Eason is still coming off the bench significantly more often than not. He is generally a wing, meaning that his most likely competition is Jabari Smith Jr. and Kenyon Martin Jr. I’m singling out Smith, who was drafted higher, but hasn’t shown that he’s still worthy of being in the starting lineup. It’s time to switch up the rotations, and I believe that begins with switching the roles of Eason and Smith.

Argument

As of me writing this article, Tari Eason is currently averaging 9 points, 5.7 rebounds, 1 assist, 1.1 steals, and 0.6 blocks a night in only 20 minutes a game. On the other hand, Smith has posted numbers of 11.8 points, 7.0 rebounds, 1.2 assists, 0.5 steals, and 1 block per game in 30 minutes a night. Clearly, Jabari has the better counting stats on paper, but that’s to be expected considering he’s been given more opportunity throughout the season. The main point I’m trying to make here is their efficiency. Eason is shooting 45% from the field, 35.7% from 3, and 75% from the free throw line. For reference, Smith’s percentages are 38.6%, 29.1%, and 78% respectively. That’s a big difference, if you ask me.

Now I know the classic counter-argument is that Eason plays against backup units from other teams while Jabari usually faces the opposing starters. This is most likely true, but why not let Smith go against those weaker backup units to regain his confidence and actually start to positively impact games? On the contrary, we get to see if Eason’s numbers are indeed scalable and whether or not we have a true starting-caliber wing moving forwards. It seems like a win-win situation to me. The starting 5 desperately needs a new level of intensity and effort, which is something Eason can bring. He knows how to affect the game without the ball in his hands, so that wouldn’t be an issue either.

Lastly, there’s just no reason not to. At this point in the year, we simply have nothing to lose. We know Jabari alongside Alperen Sengun just doesn’t work, yet we continue to try it repeatedly. It gets very frustrating as a fan to watch. Jabari has the worst cumulative +/- on the entire team at -472. That basically means other teams have outscored the Rockets by 472 points in total when he’s on the court. For comparison, Tari’s is -135. Obviously some of that gap is just due to the minutes difference between them, but that’s still shocking. In fact, Jabari ranks 2nd worst in the entire league only to Keldon Johnson, who is basically the “star” player on the worst team in the league. We have the worst starter in basketball, and a bench player who has shown tons of flashes. Now is the time to switch those two around.

Final Thoughts

A question has come up recently about what would make this final stretch of games worth it to the franchise. The easy answer everyone gives is experimentation and changes, but they don’t make any specific suggestions after that. I have now offered an example of what can be done, and provided evidence for my reasoning. I doubt anything will happen, but I would certainly welcome it. We’ve now seen that what we’re doing doesn’t work. As a result, it’s time to literally pivot and try something new. That’s what you’re supposed to do as an NBA team. We finally moved on from Eric Gordon to open up a spot for Kenyon Martin Jr., and now it’s time to do the same for Tari Eason. Both have earned their spot, and we need Jabari to do the same. 19-year-olds especially need to be rewarded in steps. In hindsight, he should’ve never started in the first place. Now we’ve unfortunately set a precedent that cannot be continued for the sake of the organization. If nothing is done soon, it could be detrimental to our future.
