The Houston Rockets Are Fully Embracing the Tank; Or Are They?

The 2022–2023 NBA season is winding down, and the Houston Rockets should be looking towards the draft lottery after being officially eliminated from playoff contention a few weeks ago. However, the team is seemingly trying to win every possible game, holding a 3-game streak at the time of writing. There is some value in giving our young guys confidence going into next season, but every victory at this point feels bittersweet because we are also potentially rising in the standings at the same time. Below is a list of the pros and cons of each, as well as my stance on what should be happening.

Cons

The biggest con, obviously, is draft lottery positioning. The Rockets are currently the 3rd worst team in the NBA, ahead of both the San Antonio Spurs and Detroit Pistons. That gives them the following odds to land each pick (rounded): 1 - 14%, 2 - 13.4%, 3 - 12.7%, 4 - 12%, 5 - 14.8%, 6 - 26%, 7 - 7%. For reference, here are the odds for the worst team in the league: 1 - 14%, 2 - 13.4%, 3 - 12.7%, 4 - 12%, 5 - 47.9%. As you can see, the percentages don’t change for picks 1-4, but the floor drops from 5 all the way to 7. Just imagine if Houston did drop all the way to 7. All the misery we went through for 75% of the games would amount to a prospect like Cam Whitmore. I don’t mean any disrespect, but everybody knows Whitmore is likely not going to be a franchise-altering talent.

Even the other option of trading the pick becomes significantly less viable when that pick is #8 instead of #5. Notice how I said #8 instead of #7. That’s because the Rockets also have a legitimate chance of surpassing the Charlotte Hornets before the end of the 82nd game. Charlotte’s record is 22-50, while Houston’s is 18-52. That’s only a 4-win difference with 12 games to play. We have currently won 5 out of our last 8 games. If we apply that pace to the remaining 12, that’s another 7 to 8 wins on the season. Even if the Hornets don’t lose out, they can still pass us. It’s extremely worrying to think about. Granted, I doubt we’ll keep up this pace, but that’s still something worth nothing. By that point, we’re looking at Nick Smith Jr. or Keyonte George, even further down in the order.

Pros

I’ll start with the fact that it feels good to root for your team and have them come out with a win. The fanbase becomes more active, engaged, and larger when the Rockets start to look like a more competent NBA team. It shows that we do actually have real talent that can work together, not just a random group of average players with the only goal being to lose. Also, we can start to see flashes of what the future could look like. It’s hard to envision a positive outcome when we're blown out by 20 points a night and going on several 10+ game losing streaks. That just isn’t a healthy environment either. Players can more easily anger each other, coaches display a lack of effort, and even the energy inside the arena can become toxic. At the end of the day, basketball is supposed to be fun, and, for most, that “fun” aspect centers around winning.

Another pro is that the Rockets won’t become the laughing stock of the league. Opposing teams will actually start having to game-plan for and try against a team that they didn’t have to before. For example, we saw the Lakers rest Anthony Davis thinking that Houston would be an easy win, only for that decision to blow up in their face. Additionally, if Houston becomes more respected throughout this last stretch, it may lead to better chances at free-agent signings in the off-season. Just think about it: the type of guys you desire on the open market don’t want to be associated with the organization that has been the worst team in the league for 3 consecutive years. We need a sense of pride and culture that convinces everyone that there is indeed real structure and direction with this team. Over the recent months, there has been so much criticism that winning these seemingly meaningless games may be the only way to regain our reputation.

My Stance

I think I fall more into the category of wanting the Rockets to be as bad as possible for the remaining games. We’ve basically done the hard part to get ourselves into the position of having the best lottery odds and are just throwing it away with no real care. All the pros make sense, yes, but in my eyes, the damage has already been done. Picking up a few meaningless wins at the end of the season isn’t going to change our national perception. By now, people have stopped watching because there’s simply no reason to. It is also hindering the potential of some of our lower-tier young players. These guys are being given fewer and fewer minutes in an attempt for the starters to will the team to victory. That should not be the focus of this season. It’s clear Stephen Silas is done with the rebuild and is now just trying to save his job or potentially make finding a new one easier. Now is not the time to give up on the plan to bottom out. Although it does bring hope to see this group win, we are so close to the end that it just isn’t worth it anymore.
