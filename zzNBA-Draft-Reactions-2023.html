2023 NBA Draft Reactions: Recap + Takeways Moving Forward

The 2023 NBA Draft has come and gone. We are finally at a point where the speculation and deliberation can cease regarding which prospects to take or which picks to trade. In the end, Houston walked away with Amen Thompson from the Overtime Elite and Cam Whitmore from Villanova at picks 4 and 20 respectively. What went right? What went wrong? And what’s the Rockets next step forward? Following Thursday night, we gained a new level of insight into the answers to those questions. Below are my reactions and thoughts on the matter as well as how it all affects the team moving forward.

Draft Recap

Amen Thompson seemed like the obvious pick. The 6’7” guard had been mocked to the Rockets on almost every NBA big board. If the first 3 ended up being Victor Wembanyama, Brandon Miller, and Scoot Henderson, which they were, Amen was Houstons’ guy at number 4. I personally think it was the right pick, and many fans agree. He is probably the highest-upside player outside of Victor Wembenyama, and certainly has the drive and determination to see his potential realized. Even compared to his brother, Ausar Thompson, Amen gave off much more confidence and swagger on draft night. He clearly has big goals and knows who he is as a player; two things that often propel young talent forward in their careers.

Cam Whitmore, the other selection, wasn’t nearly as expected. With many mock drafts having him comfortably in the top 10, if not the top 5, seeing him fall all the way to number 20 was a huge surprise. I believe Whitmore will be the steal of the draft. That is, of course, if he can stay healthy. The big reason he fell was medical reasons, but I’m going to place more trust in our front office and medical staff that they made the right decision than any of the other 29 teams. Rafael Stone made the right pick here too. Having somebody that reports suggested Houston was considering at number 4 to be available at 20, was too good of an opportunity to pass up. Now we get to see Whitmore play with the biggest chip on his shoulder and a “watch me” mentality on one of the most athletic, exciting teams in the league. It’s an absolute win.

Big Picture

With the draft over, we can start to figure out who will and won’t be a part of the roster heading into next season. Let’s just take a look at the guards, for example. I’d assume currently that Amen Thompson and Jalen Green are going to be the starting point guard and shooting guard respectively. Off the bench, that leaves Kevin Porter Jr. and likely another guard from free agency. As a result, guys like Josh Christopher, Tyty Washington Jr., and Daishen Nix won’t receive any playing time next season if coach Ime Udoka decides to stick to a 10-man rotation. Same thing for the forwards. With the starters being Cam Whitmore and Jabari Smith Jr., that leaves Tari Eason, Jae’Sean Tate, Kenyon Martin Jr., Usman Garuba, and anyone else from free agency to compete for 2 rotational spots.

Sooner or later, fans are going to come to the realization that not all 9 first round picks from the past 3 seasons will be meaningful pieces moving forward. With $60 million dollars in cap space likely equating to 3 solid role players, that already puts us at 12 players to fit into a total of 240 minutes before factoring in the others like Kevin Porter Jr., Kenyon Martin Jr., and Jae’Sean Tate. Which players are the organization ready to move on from after just, at most, 3 seasons with the team? That question will have to be answered over the coming weeks and months. Regardless, a 5-man lineup of Amen Thompson, Jalen Green, Cam Whitmore, Jabari Smith Jr., and Alperen Sengun sounds ridiculous on paper. The Rockets are almost certainly going to be one of the most athletic teams in the league for years to come.

Final Thoughts

Rafael Stone nailed this draft. He took the best player available at each pick, and wasn’t pressured into giving away too many assets in a trade. However, the draft is only 1 piece of the puzzle. The Rockets hired the best coach and made the right picks last Thursday, but we’ve yet to see any trades or free agent signings. It’s important to take it one step at a time, but the front office should remember that they need to build a coherent roster, not just acquire talent. This team still has very little shooting. Amen Thompson certainly didn’t help and Whitmore is average at best. Hopefully, there’s still a plan in place to address that major concern. I’m really intrigued to see what’s next for this team. I know I keep saying this, but big changes are coming, and they’re coming quickly. Be prepared.
