Assessing the Houston Rockets' Recent Draft Picks As of 2023: Who Will Be Here Long-Term?

The Houston Rockets have been one of the most active teams when it comes to the NBA draft in the past couple seasons. After not having a first round pick in any draft since 2015, Sam Dekker at pick #18, they have totaled 7 players when you combine the results from 2021 and 2022. That's the most in the league, to the best of my knowledge, and, as a result, some difficult decisions are going to need to be made as time progresses. It simply isn’t realistic to have that many young players on a single roster and expect to win a Championship. We see this cycle all the time; where you package a few promising pieces for someone already in their prime. So, in preparation for the upcoming, hopefully exciting, off-season, we need to evaluate the talent that we have accumulated through the draft up to this point.

Jalen Green

Everybody knows Jalen Green, and yet he has faced nothing but criticism from the national media since entering the NBA. He’s a flashy guard that can score on pretty much anyone. Not to mention, his athletic ability is nearly unmatched, even among other NBA players. Green is currently averaging 21.7 points, 3.8 rebounds, 3.6 assists, 0.3 blocks, and 0.7 steals per game on 33.6% from 3 and 41.4% from the field this season. That’s not great, but not terrible either. He’s also having to deal with being the #1 guy that opposing teams game plan for on a night-to-night basis. Although his struggles with passing, defense, shooting, and overall consistency are a bit concerning, I still have complete faith in Green's future. While he may never be the top guy on a championship team, I would be extremely pleased with him as a second option further down the line. Definitely a keeper in my eyes.

Alperen Sengun

Alperen Sengun has been labeled as a diamond in the rough by most outsider perspectives. Now, I’m not saying they’re wrong. Sengun has been very impressive. He’s currently averaging 14.8 points, 8.8 rebounds, 3.8 assists, 0.9 blocks, and 0.8 steals a night on 31.8% from 3 and 55.6% from the field in approximately 28 minutes. He converts some amazing passes on a consistent basis and is one of the craftiest players in the NBA. However, there are still major flaws in his game, like with basically everyone on the roster. Defense being the most obvious one. He struggles considerably when trying to guard bigger opposing centers, and doesn’t have the inside presence to stop many guards from converting on easy layups. The organization doesn’t seem to value his skillset very much either. He’s the type of guy who needs the proper team around him to succeed, and I doubt that’ll come to fruition in Houston. He has enormous potential, so really just a wait-and-see game at this point.

Usman Garuba

Garuba has received a lot of criticism both within and outside the Houston Rockets’ fanbase, and I believe most of it is unwarranted. People tend to forget that he was selected as more of a project pick that would take several years to develop. We’re currently only in year 2, and in year 1, Garuba barely received any minutes. He’s currently averaging just 3.3 points, 4.4 rebounds, 0.9 assists, 0.4 blocks, and 0.7 steals a game on 55.3% from 3 and 54.0% from the field. Those shooting splits look insane, but the 3-point percentage especially is from a small sample size, and is, most likely, not sustainable. I really wish we got to see him more than just 13 minutes a game on some nights for a better conclusion to be drawn. Anyways, I think we need to hold onto Garuba. If he can develop at least somewhat of an offensive skill set, then he’ll be a solid rotational player. Pairing him with the Goon Squad earlier this year showed flashes of what could happen with him on the floor, and it gave reason to trust that there’s still something there.

Josh Christopher

Josh Christopher has had a very intriguing career so far. He was one of the best players from the later first round last year, and then seemingly fell out of the rotation overnight. I think a majority of people have turned against Christopher lately, and I’m not quite sure why. He’s taken full advantage of every opportunity on the court this year. Christopher is currently averaging 5.1 points, 1.0 assists, 1.1 rebounds, 0.4 steals, and 0.3 blocks per game on 45.8% from the field and a dreadful 24.6% from 3. Right now, he can’t shoot from beyond the arc whatsoever, but neither can anybody else on the roster, so that shouldn’t really be held against him. One thing he can do, though, is score. I think if he was just given starter-level minutes he would easily average 15+ points per game. Unfortunately, that’s just not what we need right now. Again, I wish we had more data to look at because we need to know if he could ever evolve into something more. He just needs more time to fully unlock his potential, yet I don’t think that time will be granted.

Jabari Smith Jr.

Jabari Smith Jr. has had a rough rookie campaign, but is seemingly starting to pick it up. He’s our most recent top-level prospect to date, and his development could be crucial to our future. Smith is currently averaging 11.8 points, 6.9 rebounds, 1.2 assists, 0.5 steals, and 0.9 blocks on 38.9% from the field and 29.6% from 3. You’ve probably heard it a million times: those aren’t great shooting splits for a guy coming into the draft labeled as a shooter. I do think the other parts of his game often go unnoticed, though, because of how much everybody is focusing on the shooting struggles. Above all, his defense has been good when the proper effort is given. Smith has the ability to lock down some of the league’s best players already, at only 19 years old, and that’s only going to get better throughout his career. His rebounding isn’t bad either. That being said, he’s not able to create many shots for himself or teammates, or impact the game inside of the paint. The former #3 overall pick just doesn’t have the strength required to be a threat in the post; sadly, that’s not something I see changing anytime soon either.

Tari Eason

Tari Eason is probably the person given the most credit on the entire roster, and it’s for good reason. He has been absolutely stellar since Summer League. Eason is currently averaging 9.2 points, 5.7 rebounds, 1.0 assists, 1.2 steals, and 0.6 blocks per game on 35.7% from 3 and 45.4% from the field. Even though his efficiency isn’t as good as it could be, it’s a lot better than most other guys in the rotation. He also plays with a level of hustle and intensity which is difficult to find on a team as bad as the Rockets 65 games into the season. However, one factor that is easy to forget is that the former 17th overall pick turns 22 in just 2 months. That would make him a college senior. For comparison, Usman Garuba, who came into the league a year earlier, is turning 21 on March 9th. How good do we think Jabari will be in 2 or more years? If we look at it from that perspective, then Eason’s performance seems like it should’ve been expected. I do like his playstyle and think he’ll have a long career in the NBA as a high-energy guy off the bench. I’m just excited to see what else he can add to his game this summer.

Tyty Washington Jr.

Tyty Washington Jr. was the last player to come from our draft haul in 2022. He actually fell a lot further down in the draft order than most people were expecting, and the Rockets are lucky to have him. Washington is currently averaging 4.7 points, 1.4 rebounds, 1.6 assists, 0.4 steals, and 0.1 blocks per game on 22.4% from 3 and 35.9% from the field. Yet another guy who can’t shoot very well this season. It appears to be a common trend. Even so, Washington has been decent for the Rockets. Not great, but certainly not terrible either. He is able to impact the game in so many ways off the bench, being the only “true” point guard on the entire roster. The fact that he isn’t receiving more playing time baffles me to this day. The former Kentucky Wildcat is also a bit older, like Eason, for a rookie. That’s why I don’t know how much time he has left in the league. What happens when there’s no longer a team in desperate need of a playable backup PG. He needs to add more to his skillset to make it much farther, in my opinion.
